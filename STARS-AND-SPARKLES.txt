NOTES ON STARS & SPARKLES:

These patterns capture various stellar phenomena:

★ = Primary star (brightest, most stable)
● = Super-bright core or main focus
◆ = Secondary sparkle or dimmer star
○ = Faint, barely visible
░ = Glow halo or diffuse light
✧ = Optional extra sparkle

Stars twinkle due to atmospheric distortion. In ASCII, we
simulate this through:
- Radiating patterns (brightness spreading outward)
- Pulsing rings (concentric layers)
- Cascading sparkles (light trailing downward)
- Halos (glow surrounding the star)

Some patterns are meant for static display. Others suggest
temporal change—a star brightening, fading, or moving.

The solitary beacon (SPARKLE 20) demonstrates the power of
negative space: a single star surrounded by emptiness carries
more weight than any crowded cluster.
