  SECTION 10 — WHY THIS MATTERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Emergence is not just physics or biology. It's the principle that guides
design, composition, and creation.

Every system we build—visual, narrative, organizational—follows the same
pattern:

    Individual elements → Rules of interaction → Complex behavior emerges

This is why Bauhaus worked. Why good design feels inevitable. Why
beauty surprises us: it emerges from simplicity.

ACT 2 — PRINCIPLES AT WORK

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

In this version, typography drives the composition. ASCII art supports
the narrative, not the reverse.

Notice:
• Heavy headers (═══) open major sections—visual anchors
• Light headers (───) bridge ideas—transitions
• Blank lines create rhythm—breathing
• The narrative asks questions—engagement
• ASCII art illustrates ideas, not replaces them

This is typography as primary design element. The words, headers, and
spacing create the structure. The visual elements serve the story.

Each section header identifies its position and theme:
"SECTION 1 — THE VOID SPEAKS" tells you where you are and what you're
about to experience.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Typography Principles Applied:
• Headers as Hierarchy: Heavy (═══) signal major sections
• Typography as Structure: Text organizes without needing elaborate ASCII
• Narrative Flow: Each section asks or answers
• Breathing Room: Blank lines signal pauses
• Typography for Tonal Shifts: Light headers mark reflective moments
• Section Identification: Every section clearly numbered and named
• Typography as Balance: Spare text after dense sections
• Form Follows Function: Every typographic choice serves the narrative
