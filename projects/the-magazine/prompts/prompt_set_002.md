## Issue 002 — 2026-05-06

CULTURAL THREAD: The Newsstand, 1958–1975 — the designed surface of everyday commercial life at the corner store: daily newspapers, music press, cigarette packs, candy wrappers, matchbook covers, transit transfers, baseball cards, detergent boxes. The products of graphic culture, examined from the rack outward.
GRID LOGIC: Four rows tracking the cultural register from editorial (spreads) through product (packaging) to ephemera — aspect ratios shift from portrait to horizontal to ultra-wide and back, creating visual tension across the 3×4 grid. The 23:14 Life spread at row 3, col 1 disrupts the row rhythm deliberately.
ARC: American broadsheet front page (the editorial anchor) — newsstand product registers (packaging, ephemera, editorial in rotation) — American commercial compression (gum, baseball card, detergent) — newspaper classifieds back page (the opposite face of the opening, the grid laid bare)
