# Module Proposal: Packaging
## Proposed by: The Editor
## Date: 2026-05-06
## Session: Module development session (post-Issue 001)

---

WHAT IT IS:
Commercial product packaging from the mass-market retail register, 1955–1995 — cereal boxes, matchbook covers, soap labels, cigarette packs, canned food labels, candy wrappers, detergent boxes — the designed surface of the commodity, where graphic culture meets the supermarket shelf. Packaging surrounds a product with panels and structural logic; ephemera documents a transaction. The distinction is the container vs. the record.

WHY IT BELONGS:
objects.md covers the graphic production object (the tool, the instrument, the designed print artifact as aesthetic object); packaging is a different cultural register entirely — the designed surface exists to sell a product at retail, not to be appreciated as graphic design, which means its visual logic is driven by product name prominence, color-coding for shelf differentiation, compressed information hierarchy, and the aesthetics of mass production rather than limited-edition print — none of which objects.md specifies or can produce with accuracy.

EVIDENCE FROM COLLECTION:
Alfred's brief names "social living" and "pop culture" as collection territory — the current module layer cannot touch either. A 1967 breakfast cereal box is social living. A 1975 cigarette pack is pop culture. Both are graphic culture artifacts that the collection's current modules cannot address. The archive has zero entries from the retail shelf after 13 approved images.

EXAMPLE PROMPT:

NEVER LIST DRAFT:
- Never luxury, artisanal, or high-end product packaging — this is always mass-market, supermarket shelf, corner store register
- Never packaging that reads as contemporary, digital-native, or post-1995 design — the register is 1955–1995, pre-digital packaging production
- Never dimensional rendering of the package as a three-dimensional object — the composition is always the flat graphic panel as printed
- Never ephemera (tickets, stamps, receipts) — packaging contains; ephemera documents a transaction
- Never objects.md territory — this module never produces the production tool or instrument, only the surface designed to sell the product
