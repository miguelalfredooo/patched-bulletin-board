## The Frame

The Magazine™'s visual collection curates images and objects across five editorial territories that reflect how creative people live and work:

- **Space** — Creative environments, studios, workspaces, homes
- **Sound** — Music, listening, the visual cultures around sound
- **Practice** — Tools, craft, the discipline of making
- **Scene** — Subcultures, movements, underground networks
- **Table** — Food, ritual, the social life of gathering

Every piece serves one of these pillars. Every piece is grounded in a specific moment, place, and culture — not a generic aesthetic.

---

## What Matters

- **Specificity** — "1974 concert poster from a Berkeley basement club" not "vintage poster"
- **Grounded in reality** — It actually existed, made by real people, in a real moment
- **Surface evidence** — How something was made, worn, used tells its story
- **Curatorial intention** — Which pillar? What does it reveal about creative culture?
- **Tone** — Direct, curious, alive. Not precious, not ironic, not heavy

---

## What Doesn't Belong

- Generic aesthetic language ("retro," "vintage," "artisanal" without context)
- Anything designed-for-beauty rather than authentically grounded
- Typography unless Alfred specifically requests it
- Heavy, solemn, or contemptuous tone
- Anything that feels styled or erased of evidence

---

## Prompt Rules

**1. Aspect ratio — always vary**  
No two consecutive prompts use the same ratio.  
Approved: `1:1` · `4:5` · `5:7` · `4:3` · `16:9` · `2:1` · `23:14`

**2. Every prompt ends with:** `--chaos 20 --p m7446277342072143883`

**3. The `--p` flag is never omitted or modified.**

**4. One sentence maximum.**  
Lead with specificity: era, place, reference, which pillar, what it reveals.

**5. Every approved prompt saved as `prompts/prompt_[number].md`**

---

## Editor-In-Chief Self-Check

Before passing to Creative Director:
- [ ] Specific reference grounded in time/place/culture
- [ ] Which pillar does it serve?
- [ ] One sentence, no generic language
- [ ] Aspect ratio different from previous
- [ ] Ends with `--chaos 20 --p m7446277342072143883`
- [ ] Typography only if Alfred requested it

---

## Creative Director Gate

Before approving, ask:
- Does this belong to a specific moment and culture?
- Which pillar does it strengthen?
- What does the collection already have in this territory?
- Does this maintain the rigor and specificity of The Magazine™?
