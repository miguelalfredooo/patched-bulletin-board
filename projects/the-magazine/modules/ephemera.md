## Purpose

Small-format commercial print designed for brief functional life — postage stamps, concert tickets, bus transfers, matchbook covers, trading cards, beer labels, postmarks, receipts — the graphic culture of the object you are meant to throw away.

---

## Input contract

The Editor receives: archive state from archive-log.md, governance rules from VISUAL-DNA.md, style translation guidance from STYLE-GUIDE.md.

The Editor identifies a specific ephemeral artifact — a stamp, a ticket, a matchbook cover, a trading card — grounded in a specific print era, production technology, and cultural context. The piece is always one artifact examined alone.

---

## Output contract

One Midjourney prompt. One sentence. Ends with --chaos 20 --p m7446277342072143883. Aspect ratio matches the cultural format of the ephemeral artifact — stamps are square, tickets are horizontal, tall labels are portrait.

---

## Voice / register

Vernacular commercial print at its most compressed. Maximum information in minimum space. Functional beauty — designed for instant legibility and immediate disposal. The graphic logic is always driven by the artifact's purpose: denomination, date, destination, event, product. Not the designer's deliberate aesthetic — the commercial printer's practical solution, executed in the specific production language of its era.

---

## Always

- Flat graphic execution — no atmospheric depth, no dimensional rendering
- Specific print reference — identifiable era, technology, and cultural context
- Small-format logic — the composition fits in a hand; scale is always intimate
- Functional hierarchy — the artifact's primary information is the primary visual element
- Economy — every element serves the artifact's function, nothing decorative
- Designed object register — printed matter with a job to do, not fine art

---

## Never

- Never produce anything at poster, magazine, or large-format scale — the entire composition must fit in a hand
- Never fine art print editions, artist multiples, or gallery objects — always commercial, functional, disposable
- Never "distressed" or "aged" without naming the specific paper stock and production technology that produced the aging
- Never packaging designed to contain a product — that is a different module (packaging reads the shelf; ephemera documents a transaction)
- Never a composition without a clear production-era specific graphic logic — stamps have perforation grids, tickets have serial numbers, matchbooks have strike-surface zones
- Never multiple objects arranged as a collection or specimen display — each prompt is one ephemeral artifact examined alone

---

## Cultural register

- US and international postage stamps, 1940s–1990s
- Concert and event ticket stubs, 1960s–1980s
- Transit tokens, bus transfers, and fare cards, 1950s–1980s
- Matchbook cover design, 1940s–1970s
- Sports and entertainment trading cards, 1950s–1990s
- Beer, wine, and spirits bottle labels, 1960s–1990s
- Movie theater, theme park, and amusement park tickets
- Receipt and invoice typography, retail and commercial contexts
- Postmarks, rubber stamps, and postal cancellation marks

---

## Prompt rules

All rules from VISUAL-DNA.md apply. Summary:
- One sentence maximum
- Aspect ratio varies — match to the artifact's format: 1:1 for stamps, 2:1 or 23:14 for tickets and transfers, 4:5 for tall labels
- Always ends with: --chaos 20 --p m7446277342072143883
- --p flag never omitted, never modified
