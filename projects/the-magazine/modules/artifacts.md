# Module: Artifacts

**the-magazine**

**Version:** 2.0.0

---

## Purpose

Designed, made, or produced things captured with specificity. Tools, objects, products, printed matter, instruments, vessels, clothing — anything made by human hands or machines with intentional design.

The artifact carries its era and culture in its surface: how it was made, what it's made of, how it shows use or age.

---

## What It Serves

- **Practice** — tools worn smooth by use, instruments, craft objects
- **Sound** — vinyl sleeves, instruments, recording equipment, speakers
- **Scene** — fashion items, subcultural markers, posters, flyers, zines
- **Table** — packaging, dishware, kitchen tools, serving vessels
- **Space** — furniture, lighting, architectural elements as objects

---

## Visual Territory

- Tools and instruments (hand planes, synthesizer modules, cooking implements)
- Vinyl sleeves, album covers, CD packaging
- Printed matter (posters, flyers, zines, publications, packaging)
- Vessels and dishware (bowls, plates, cups, bento boxes)
- Clothing and accessories (worn jackets, shoes, bags)
- Furniture and household objects
- Street signs, commercial signage
- Collected or archival objects

---

## Always

- Specific reference — not "vintage tool" but "1970s Japanese hand plane with indigo dye stains"
- Surface evidence visible — wear, aging, material, production marks, use
- Grounded in a real moment and culture
- Direct and legible — reads at a glance, rewards closer looking

---

## Never

- Generic aesthetic language without specific reference
- Objects designed-for-beauty rather than authentically made
- Loss of surface evidence (pristine, over-cleaned, staged)
- Anything that erases the evidence of actual use or time
