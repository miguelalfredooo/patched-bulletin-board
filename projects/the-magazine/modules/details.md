# Module: Details

**the-magazine**

**Version:** 2.0.0

---

## Purpose

Close-ups, textures, surfaces, and material evidence examined as subject. The zoom into how something was made, worn, aged, or used. Detail reveals the craft and history embedded in physical objects.

---

## What It Serves

- **Practice** — close-ups of tools showing wear, carving marks, patina, sharpening evidence
- **Space** — architectural materials, paint layers, wood grain, light on surfaces
- **Table** — food surfaces, ceramic glaze, metal patina, material evidence of cooking
- **Artifacts** — worn edges, production marks, aging, material specificity
- **Scene** — wear patterns on walls, layered marks, community traces

---

## Visual Territory

- Close-ups of worn tool surfaces (blade edges, handles, patina)
- Textile details (weave pattern, dye, embroidery, wear)
- Paper and printing surfaces (halftone dots, ink texture, aging)
- Metal patina, rust, polishing marks
- Wood grain, carving marks, hand-plane finish
- Ceramic glaze, clay texture, firing marks
- Paint layers, wear, aging, community marks on walls
- Food preparation surfaces, stains, wear
- Architectural materials and aging (concrete, plaster, tile)
- Light on surfaces, shadow patterns

---

## Always

- Specific source — this detail from *this* object, surface, or material
- Magnification reveals truth — the zoom shows how something was made or used
- Material specificity — identify the material and process
- Scale clear — how zoomed in are we
- Cultural or temporal ground — when/where/why this matters

---

## Never

- Abstract texture without reference to source
- Magnification that obscures rather than clarifies
- Generic "texture" without specificity
- Loss of context about what the detail comes from
