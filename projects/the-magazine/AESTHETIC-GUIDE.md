# AESTHETIC-GUIDE.md — Translating Visual DNA to Midjourney Prompts

This guide translates the VISUAL-DNA.md specification into actionable Midjourney prompt language and constraints.

---

## Core Constraint Framework

Every prompt must encode these non-negotiable constraints:

### 1. Museum/Archive Register

**What it means:** The work is presentation, not action. Forms are isolated and documented, as if in a museum display or archive catalog.

**Midjourney equivalent:**
```
studio photography, museum specimen, clinical documentation, 
neutral background, isolated subject, archival aesthetic
```

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ "in nature" or "in habitat" (contextualizes, breaks archive register)
- ❌ "dramatic lighting" (adds narrative)
- ❌ "cinematic" (implies storytelling)

### 2. Sculptural Three-Dimensionality

**What it means:** All forms have depth, shadow, surface variation. Nothing is flat. Everything looks touchable and object-like.

**Midjourney equivalent:**
```
three-dimensional sculpture, dimensional form, tactile surface,
defined shadows, high detail, sculptural precision
```

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ "flat" or "2D"
- ❌ "painted" or "illustration"
- ❌ "rendered" (too abstract)

### 3. Abstraction + Biomorphic Suggestion

**What it means:** Forms are abstract enough to not be literal objects, but vague biomorphic suggestions (heads, tools, organisms, plants) that feel almost recognizable but wrong.

**Midjourney equivalent:**
```
abstract form, biomorphic, vaguely organic, ambiguous form,
semi-figurative, sculptural abstraction, uncanny geometry
```

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ "realistic" or "photorealistic"
- ❌ "detailed" (detail over-specifies)
- ❌ "clearly a [specific object]"

### 4. Formal Precision & Control

**What it means:** Lighting, composition, framing are meticulously controlled. Nothing is accidental. Symmetry and geometry are default.

**Midjourney equivalent:**
```
geometric precision, symmetrical composition, controlled lighting,
formal arrangement, perfect symmetry, mathematical form
```

**Add when symmetry is desired:**
```
bilaterally symmetrical, perfectly symmetrical, radial symmetry,
mirror composition
```

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ "organic" or "natural" (implies randomness)
- ❌ "chaotic" or "wild"

### 5. Monochromatic Base Aesthetic

**What it means:** Core aesthetic is monochromatic. Color is additive and limited, not foundational.

**Midjourney equivalent:**
```
monochromatic, grayscale, white on gray, minimal color,
desaturated, neutral tones, cool palette
```

**If adding color (mainly textures series):**
```
restrained color palette, limited color, warm and cool contrast,
complementary color relationship
```

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ "vibrant" or "saturated" (unless textures)
- ❌ "rainbow" or "multicolored"

### 6. Neutral Affect & Absence of Narrative

**What it means:** No emotion, no story, no action. Pure presence and observation. Timeless.

**Midjourney equivalent:**
```
no action, static form, timeless, absence of narrative,
clinical presentation, documentary style, neutral mood,
devoid of emotion
```

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ "dramatic" or "dynamic"
- ❌ "expressive" or "emotional"
- ❌ "movement" or "motion"
- ❌ "interacting" or "engaging"

### 7. Scale Ambiguity

**What it means:** You can't tell if something is 2 inches or 20 feet. No human figures, hands, or known objects for scale reference.

**Midjourney equivalent:**
```
no scale reference, ambiguous scale, unknown size,
no human figure, isolated object, size ambiguity
```

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ Include people, hands, animals, or known objects
- ❌ Show the piece in a landscape with clear scale context
- ❌ Use perspective cues that suggest specific size

### 8. Tactile Material Awareness

**What it means:** Surface quality, finish, material appearance matter enormously. The look of the material is part of the meaning.

**Midjourney equivalent:**
```
matte finish, sculptural surface, weathered appearance,
cast stone, carved form, textured surface, material presence
```

**By material language:**
- Stone/marble: `stone sculpture, carved stone, marble, granular surface`
- Metal: `metal casting, metallic surface, cast metal, verdigris` (avoid "shiny" or "polished")
- Ceramic/clay: `ceramic form, clay sculpture, fired surface`
- Bone/ivory: `bone-like, ivory-white, skeletal form, delicate structure`

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ "glossy" or "shiny" (too much luster)
- ❌ "smooth" alone (needs material context)

## Series-Specific Constraints

### Series I — The Objects (Artifacts, Apparatus, Forms)

**Base constraint:** Museum artifact aesthetic. Forms are tools, vessels, ritual objects, or heads—abstracted and formal.

**Prompt additions:**
```
artifact form, archaeological object, abstract artifact,
formal simplicity, minimal detail, isolated form
```

**Geometry emphasis:** These tend toward symmetry, clean lines, geometric purity.

**Scale:** Very ambiguous. Could be 3 inches or 3 feet.

**Color:** Primarily white/cream or black forms. Occasionally metallics (but never shiny).

---

### Series II — The Beings (Figurines, Face-Forms, Entity Taxonomy)

**Base constraint:** Figurine or head-like forms without expression or character. Vaguely anthropomorphic but alien.

**Prompt additions:**
```
stylized head form, figurine, abstract face, non-representational,
no expression, no character, vaguely humanoid, alien entity
```

**Key rejection:** If the form has a "face" that feels like a person or creature, it's wrong. Needs to be more alien/abstract.

**Symmetry:** Strong bilateral or radial symmetry is common here.

---

### Series III — The Signals (Inscriptions, Relief Carvings, Language Evidence)

**Base constraint:** Surfaces with patterns, marks, inscriptions—suggesting language or meaning-making without being readable.

**Prompt additions:**
```
relief carving, incised marks, patterned surface,
inscribed form, concentric patterns, coded surface
```

**Pattern types:**
- Concentric lines/circles
- Wave or ripple patterns
- Geometric marks
- Grid-like inscriptions
- Radial patterns

**Readability:** Patterns suggest meaning but never actual text or symbols.

---

### Series IV — The Sites (Ritual Landscapes, Interiors, Ruins)

**Base constraint:** Architectural or landscape-scale forms. Monuments, altars, temples, ceremonial sites.

**Prompt additions:**
```
archaeological site, monument, architectural structure,
ceremonial space, ritual landscape, megaliths, ruins,
constructed landscape
```

**Key element:** Horizon line or sense of place. Always shows context (sky, ground, surroundings).

**Symmetry:** Often symmetrical or radiating (temples, altars, ceremonial design).

**Scale:** These are landscape-scale, but still feel slightly off or too perfect to be real ancient sites.

---

### Series V — The Materials (Extreme Close-ups, Surface Texture, Tactile Studies)

**Base constraint:** This is the most permissive category. Texture, materiality, and surface are the subject. More color allowed here.

**Prompt additions:**
```
tactile surface, texture study, close-up form, material detail,
surface quality focus, tactile sculpture
```

**Color permission:** This series can have warmer palette (oranges, yellows, teals, greens) while maintaining formal precision. Still not decorative.

**Biomorphic freedom:** Forms can be more organic here while staying formal.

---

### Series VI — The Collection (Archive Assembles, Typologies, Taxonomies)

**Base constraint:** Multiple forms organized in grid or taxonomy layout. Natural history museum specimen display logic.

**Prompt additions:**
```
specimen collection, natural history display, grid layout,
cabinet of curiosities, taxonomy of forms, classified display,
specimen arrangement, array composition
```

**Arrangement:** Grid, array, or organized display. Multiple (usually 4-9) forms in one frame.

**Consistency:** All forms in the collection should feel like they belong together—same aesthetic register, roughly similar scale conceptually.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid (Universal)

These violate VISUAL-DNA "Never" qualities and should trigger prompt rewrites:

1. **Narrative or action** → "no movement," "static form," "absent action"
2. **Decoration for decoration's sake** → Remove adjectives like "beautiful," "ornate," "decorative"
3. **Hyper-realistic photographic rendering** → Use "sculpture," "form," "abstract," not "photorealistic"
4. **Busy or chaotic backgrounds** → "neutral background," "isolated," "void background"
5. **Emotional gesture or expression** → "no expression," "neutral," "devoid of emotion"
6. **Character or personality** → "abstract form," "non-representational," "alien"
7. **Multiple objects interacting** → "isolated," "singular focus," separate specimens with negative space
8. **Obvious metaphor or symbolism** → Keep forms abstract and open-ended
9. **Realistic scale** → "ambiguous scale," "no human reference," "size ambiguity"
10. **Saturated or complex color** (except textures) → "monochromatic," "desaturated," "minimal color"

## Prompt Structure Template

**For Objects/Specimens:**
```
[object type/description], [biomorphic quality], [geometric/symmetry], 
[material/surface], studio photography, museum specimen, clinical lighting,
neutral background, isolated form, monochromatic, no scale reference, 
sculptural precision, high detail
```

**For Monuments/Sites:**
```
[site type/description], [architectural/landscape quality], [symmetry/geometry],
[material/surface], [scale], archaeological aesthetic, ceremonial landscape,
horizon line present, natural lighting, neutral mood, timeless, no narrative,
formal composition, geometric precision
```

**For Organisms:**
```
[organism suggestion], [biomorphic detail], [abstract quality],
[material/finish], natural history specimen, museum display,
dark background, isolated form, clinical lighting, monochromatic,
delicate structure, sculptural form, high detail
```

**For Sites (landscape):**
```
[landscape/constructed feature], [architectural/natural quality],
[symmetry/geometry], [material], landscape archaeology, ceremonial site,
aerial or landscape perspective, horizon present, natural atmospheric lighting,
no narrative, timeless, formal arrangement, monochromatic or cool palette
```

**For Textures:**
```
[texture/surface type], [material], [biomorphic quality], close-up detail,
tactile surface, material focus, sculptural form, raking light,
[color palette - can be warmer here], formal precision, high detail, 
texture emphasis
```

## Testing the Prompt

Before submitting to Alfred, ask:

1. ✅ Does the prompt enforce museum/archive register?
2. ✅ Does it require sculptural form and defined shadows?
3. ✅ Does it encourage abstraction and biomorphic suggestion?
4. ✅ Does it avoid narrative, emotion, and decoration?
5. ✅ Does it maintain monochromatic base (or restrained color for textures)?
6. ✅ Does it create scale ambiguity?
7. ✅ Does it match the VISUAL-DNA "Always" qualities?
8. ✅ Does it avoid VISUAL-DNA "Never" qualities?

If you can answer yes to all 8, the prompt is ready.

## Examples (By Series)

### Series I — The Objects

**Good:**
```
abstract ceremonial vessel, geometric form, bilaterally symmetrical, 
cast stone sculpture, matte surface, studio photography, museum specimen,
neutral gray background, isolated artifact, no scale reference, 
monochromatic, sculptural precision, formal simplicity
```

**Bad:**
```
ancient ritual vase, beautiful ornate artifact, sitting in museum display case,
dramatic lighting highlighting fine details, vibrant natural stone colors,
storytelling aesthetic
```

### Series IV — The Sites

**Good:**
```
ceremonial landscape monument, geometric megaliths, radial symmetry,
carved stone architecture, weathered surface, ritual site aesthetic,
aerial view, horizon with sky, natural neutral lighting, no narrative,
timeless, archaeological precision, cool tone palette
```

**Bad:**
```
dramatic ancient temple ruins overgrown with vines, golden hour lighting,
epic scale showing human civilization's grandeur, cinematic composition
```

### Series V — The Textures

**Good:**
```
sculptural biomorphic surface, organic form detail, warm orange and cool teal
in complementary relationship, matte finish, close-up tactile study,
raking light defining texture, high detail, formal precision, material focus
```

**Bad:**
```
colorful abstract art, vibrant rainbow of saturated hues, playful and expressive,
emotional gesture in form, decorative surface pattern
```
