## Your Workflow

### Phase 1: Briefing (5 minutes)
1. Read the archive-log.md entry for your issue
2. Note the theme, editorial mix, sonic reference, closing sentence
3. Open CONTENT-BRIEFS-003-010.md and find your issue brief

### Phase 2: Curation (30-40 minutes)
1. For each of the 11 sections, follow the **Prose Brief** angle
2. Find one publication/work that exemplifies that angle
3. Track sources—no repeats except music
4. Verify formats vary (no consecutive A-A or E-E, etc.)

### Phase 3: Prose Development (45-60 minutes)
1. Write 1-2 sentences per section following the brief angle
2. Use PROSE-STRUCTURE-REFERENCE.md to check prose weight matches format weight
3. Ensure theme is felt, not stated
4. Maintain Apartamento register (warm, specific, editorial)

### Phase 4: Polish (10-15 minutes)
1. Check closing sentence lands thematically
2. Write cultural thread (3-4 sentences, no audience)
3. Write curator note (reflect on theme distinctions from adjacent issues)
4. Run ASSIGNMENT-EDITOR-QUICKSTART.md checklist

**Total time per issue: 90-120 minutes**

## The 11 Sections Explained

### Standard Arc Structure
```
1. OPENING (Heavy) → 2. Light Reset → 3. Building → 4. Deepening
→ 5. Pause → 6. Shift → 7. Climax Approach → 8. Hold Before Peak
→ 9. Climactic Peak → 10. Resolution → 11. Closing (Minimal)
```

Each section has a **function**, a **primary format**, and a **thematic anchor**. See PROSE-STRUCTURE-REFERENCE.md for full mapping.

### Format Assignments (A-E)
- **A** — Sparse Object, Light (1-3 simple lines; minimal prose)
- **B** — Geometric Frame, Heavy (bounded, framed, dense)
- **C** — Typographic, Medium (thinking-oriented, perspective shift)
- **D** — Two Column, Medium (contrasts, building complexity)
- **E** — Full Spread, Very Heavy (maximum density, climax weight)

**Key rule:** No consecutive sections repeat the same format.

## Prose Writing Principles

### Register (Tone/Voice)
**Apartamento register:** Intimate, unhurried, never trend-chasing. The writing notices the ashtray on the table, not just the room. Warm without being soft, specific without being academic.

### Specificity
Every sentence should be about **this** work, **this** moment, **this** gesture—not themes in general.

❌ Poor: "This designer is exploring the tension between process and outcome."
✅ Good: "Sherald's raw linen holds the grisaille, aging as the paint dries."

### Theme Invisibility
The theme should **never be stated** in any section prose. It should be **felt** through:
- The specificity of selections
- The word choice and rhythm
- The visual form
- The convergence of examples (without explaining the convergence)

✅ Good (theme felt, not stated):
- Section on Sherald: "Raw linen holds the grisaille"
- Section on Brown: "Figures dissolve into ground"
- Section on Yanagi: "Unsigned pots, the user's hand completes it"
- Closing: "The things worth keeping are the things that show you used them."

❌ Bad (theme stated):
- "This work is about wear and time-in-contact"
- "These artists explore the concept of wearing"
- "Each section celebrates how things age"

### Rhythm & Pacing
Prose rhythm should vary across sections and match the format weight:
- **Heavy sections (E, B):** Longer, more complex sentences; active verbs; dense imagery
- **Medium sections (D, C):** Balanced; building; bridging
- **Light sections (A):** Short, spare, often poetic; minimal punctuation

### Length Guidelines
- **Heavy sections:** 2-3 sentences (sometimes more)
- **Medium sections:** 2 sentences (balanced)
- **Light sections:** 1-2 sentences (often just 1)
- **Very heavy sections (climax):** Can be 2-4 sentences; can build

## Editorial Mix Modulation

The **Editorial Mix** percentages should modulate your prose choices:

### Music (0-100%)
- **0%:** Prose ignores sonic qualities
- **50%:** Sonic reference is contextual mood-setting
- **75-100%:** Every section mirrors sonic rhythm, tempo, frequency, production texture

*Example at Music 75%:* Sparse piano reference (ECM) → prose should be deliberate, not rushed, with room for silence in the sentences.

### Visual (0-100%)
- **0%:** ASCII is decorative; prose drives structure
- **50%:** Visual and prose are equal partners
- **75-100%:** Visual composition determines section order; prose follows visual momentum

*Example at Visual 90%:* Composition is primary; sections reordered to follow visual rhythm; prose supports visual weight.

### Research (0-100%)
- **0%:** Surface-level curation only
- **50%:** 2-3 signal layers, obvious connections
- **100%:** Deep source synthesis, 4-5 convergence layers, novel combinations

### Process (0-100%)
- **0%:** Maker perspective absent
- **50%:** Process is contextual
- **75-100%:** "How is this made?" centers every selection

### Theme (0-100%)
- **0%:** Invisible; felt only through aesthetic coherence
- **50%:** Theme is contextual but not explicit
- **75-100%:** Theme is woven into prose; visible across all sections

### AI Culture (0-100%)
- **0%:** Tools and AI absent
- **50%:** AI references are contextual
- **75-100%:** AI/tool philosophy featured prominently

## Checklist Before Submission

**Prose Quality**
- [ ] Each section is specific to the curated work, not generic to theme
- [ ] Theme is felt, not stated (check: could you remove the theme word and still feel it?)
- [ ] Prose rhythm varies across sections
- [ ] Visual weight matches format assignment (A = light prose, E = heavy prose)
- [ ] Register is Apartamento (warm, specific, editorial)

**Structure**
- [ ] No two consecutive formats are identical
- [ ] Climactic peak is at Section 9 (or justified elsewhere)
- [ ] Light resets at Sections 2, 5, 8, 11 create breathing room
- [ ] Pacing builds from opening through climax to resolution

**Sourcing**
- [ ] All 11 sections use different sources (except music)
- [ ] Sources align with approved publications per section
- [ ] No source repeats within the issue
- [ ] Music sources noted (can repeat)

**Thematic Integrity**
- [ ] Closing sentence lands without being preachy
- [ ] Cultural thread connects examples without over-explaining
- [ ] Curator note acknowledges what this issue is *not* (distinction from adjacent themes)
- [ ] Editorial mix modulations are evident in prose choices

## Common Pitfalls & Fixes

### Pitfall 1: Explaining the Theme
**❌ Bad:** "This issue explores imperfection as a deliberate stance."
**✅ Good:** "The split nib line is unrepeatable; no two letters match in the same sentence."

*Fix:* Show the theme through specific examples, not explanations.

### Pitfall 2: Generic Prose
**❌ Bad:** "This designer is interested in the intersection of craft and technology."
**✅ Good:** "Hand-coded design systems in CSS, not Figma—the constraint becomes the method."

*Fix:* Ground every sentence in specificity. What is this specific work doing?

### Pitfall 3: Format Weight Mismatch
**❌ Bad:** Format A (sparse, light) paired with dense, complex prose
**✅ Good:** Format A prose is short, spare, often a single strong image

*Fix:* Match prose density to format weight.

### Pitfall 4: Source Repeats
**❌ Bad:** Using Artsy for both Art and Painting sections
**✅ Good:** Track sources; when repeating, swap for alternative (It's Nice That, Booooooom, etc.)

*Fix:* Keep source checklist visible during curation.

### Pitfall 5: Weak Closing Sentence
**❌ Bad:** "Things can be worn and still be valuable."
**✅ Good:** "The things worth keeping are the things that show you used them."

*Fix:* Closing should be singular, poetic, thematically weighted. Test it: does it land?
