# ACT 2 Prose Structure Reference
## 11-Section Framework with Format & Thematic Anchor Points

Visual guide to the 11-section structure used across all issues. Each section has a primary function, optimal format assignment, and thematic anchor point.

---

## Section Assignments by Function

### SECTION 1: Opening Declaration
**Typical Formats:** E (Heavy Full Spread), B (Geometric Frame)
**Function:** Announces the issue's visual and thematic weight. Heavyweight opening. The masthead and first gesture.
**Prose Role:** Sets tone and visual register. Introduces the theme's central gesture without naming it.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** The split nib as opening gesture
- **Traces:** Fingerprints in design as forensic opening
- **Signal:** Bold geometry announcing clarity
- **Momentum:** Fast-moving practice at scale
- **Threshold:** Figure at the edge (opening)
- **Material:** Substance as irreducible fact
- **Worn:** Sherald's raw linen as material evidence

---

### SECTION 2: First Repose / Light Reset
**Typical Formats:** A (Sparse Object, Light)
**Function:** Breathing room after heavy opening. Visual and pacing reset.
**Prose Role:** Shift register slightly. Introduce secondary angle. Keep it minimal, specific.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Photography or illustration moment
- **Traces:** Sketch marks or hidden authorship
- **Signal:** Icon-like clarity moment
- **Momentum:** Rapid iteration as practice
- **Threshold:** Light crossing a surface
- **Material:** Paper as protagonist
- **Worn:** Dissolution through use

---

### SECTION 3: Building / Medium Establishment
**Typical Formats:** D (Two Column), C (Typographic)
**Function:** Mid-range visual and conceptual complexity. Moving the narrative forward.
**Prose Role:** Deeper conceptual work. Can introduce paradox or nuance.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Paint as trace of gesture
- **Traces:** Surface as archive
- **Signal:** Color as communication
- **Momentum:** Gestural speed (painters)
- **Threshold:** Portal moments (illustration)
- **Material:** Paint as substance
- **Worn:** Worn original art

---

### SECTION 4: Deepening / Heavy Build
**Typical Formats:** B (Geometric Frame), E (Full Spread)
**Function:** Approaching conceptual climax. Visual weight increasing.
**Prose Role:** Most complex thematic work. Introduce tension or contradiction.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Coil-built clay and hand presence
- **Traces:** Forensic art history analysis
- **Signal:** Form as argument (sculpture)
- **Momentum:** Dynamic forms suggesting velocity
- **Threshold:** Nengudi's loaded mesh (tension)
- **Material:** Clay and soil as architecture
- **Worn:** Nengudi's mesh showing strain

---

### SECTION 5: Pause / Minimal Reset
**Typical Formats:** A (Sparse Object, Light)
**Function:** Critical breathing point. Reset after buildup. Moment of silence.
**Prose Role:** Single image, single observation. Minimal prose. Often one sentence.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Making visible (process work)
- **Traces:** Passing work forward (handoff)
- **Signal:** Clear communication (product)
- **Momentum:** Quick prototyping test
- **Threshold:** Figma Variables (decision point)
- **Material:** Paper prototyping moment
- **Worn:** Objects in use (historical)

---

### SECTION 6: Shift / Typographic Mid-Point
**Typical Formats:** C (Typographic, Medium)
**Function:** Tonal shift or perspective change. Often introduces thinking/philosophy.
**Prose Role:** Can be more abstract, more writerly. Introduces meta-level.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Slowness case (opinion)
- **Traces:** Attention maps (AI visibility)
- **Signal:** Against ambient (clarity essay)
- **Momentum:** Tool velocity essay
- **Threshold:** Decision-making at threshold
- **Material:** Analogue resistance essay
- **Worn:** Tools that wear in (opinion)

---

### SECTION 7: Ascending Climax
**Typical Formats:** E (Dense Radiating), B (Geometric)
**Function:** Building toward peak. Visual weight increasing. Conceptual intensity rising.
**Prose Role:** Can be more poetic, more compressed. Rhythm tightens.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Typography of imperfection (brand)
- **Traces:** Visible authorship claims (brand)
- **Signal:** Logo systems (brand)
- **Momentum:** Brand velocity / trend response
- **Threshold:** Transition animations (brand)
- **Material:** Figma prototyping limits
- **Worn:** Design systems that age

---

### SECTION 8: Pause Before Peak
**Typical Formats:** A (Sparse), C (Typographic)
**Function:** Hold before climax. Suspension of momentum.
**Prose Role:** Brief, poised, waiting. Often shorter sentences.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Design & AI Tools (hand-coded)
- **Traces:** Design & AI Tools (version control)
- **Signal:** Design & AI Tools (tokens)
- **Momentum:** AI model releases / Figma speed
- **Threshold:** MVP as threshold (product)
- **Material:** Design systems aging (tools)
- **Worn:** Durability and waste (product)

---

### SECTION 9: Climactic Peak
**Typical Formats:** E (Full Expansion, Very Heavy)
**Function:** The issue's thematic and visual climax. Maximum density and weight.
**Prose Role:** Most expansive. Can build across multiple sentences. Peak intensity.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Lo-fi as movement (culture)
- **Traces:** Photography as indexical evidence
- **Signal:** High-contrast photography
- **Momentum:** Speed culture / FOMO / acceleration
- **Threshold:** Kawauchi's light photography
- **Material:** Morelos clay architecture / film grain
- **Worn:** Fukase's ravens (photography)

---

### SECTION 10: Resolution / Coming Down
**Typical Formats:** D (Two Column), C (Typographic)
**Function:** Descending from peak. Resolution returning. Visual weight decreasing.
**Prose Role:** Can be reflective, conclusive. Often shorter than climax.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Product & Process (maker interviews)
- **Traces:** Art History (craft lineages)
- **Signal:** Art History (Constructivism)
- **Momentum:** Art History (Futurism)
- **Threshold:** Art History (Rückenfigur)
- **Material:** Craft traditions (lineages)
- **Worn:** Historical objects in use

---

### SECTION 11: Closing / Minimal Resolution
**Typical Formats:** A (Sparse Object), C (Typographic)
**Function:** Graceful exit. Return to minimal forms. Final statement.
**Prose Role:** Often single image or poetic compression. Landing statement.
**Example Angles:**
- **Handmade:** Visual & Brand (typography variation)
- **Traces:** Opinion (archival thinking)
- **Signal:** Visual & Brand (marking systems)
- **Momentum:** Product & Process (sprint methodologies)
- **Threshold:** Culture / liminality in ritual
- **Material:** Packaging materiality (brand)
- **Worn:** Vintage and patina (brand aging)

## Format Sequence Principles

### Weight Distribution
The 11 sections should vary in visual weight, creating natural breathing room:
- Light (A): Sparse, minimal, one gesture
- Medium-Light (C): Typographic, readable, thinking
- Medium (D): Two-column, balanced, building
- Heavy (B): Geometric, framed, dense
- Very Heavy (E): Full spread, maximum density

### Natural Rhythm
No two consecutive sections should be the same weight. Example good pattern:
```
1:E (heavy) → 2:A (light) → 3:D (medium) → 4:B (heavy) 
→ 5:A (light) → 6:C (medium) → 7:E (heavy) → 8:A (light) 
→ 9:E (very heavy) → 10:D (medium) → 11:A (light)
```

This creates a breathing curve: opening heavy, reset light, build medium, climax heavy, reset light, etc.

### Climax Positioning
The highest visual weight (E) should appear at strategic points:
- **Section 1:** Opening declaration
- **Section 7:** Building toward climax
- **Section 9:** Peak climax
- Other E placements optional but rare

## Prose Development Across the Arc

### Pacing by Section

**Sections 1-3:** Introduce Register
- Use longer, more exploratory sentences
- Establish tone and sonic register
- Can be more narrative

**Sections 4-6:** Develop Tension
- Sentences tighten slightly
- Paradox and complication introduced
- Perspective shifts

**Sections 7-9:** Climb to Peak
- Sentences compress
- Rhythm accelerates
- Imagery intensifies
- Density increases

**Sections 10-11:** Resolution
- Breathing room returns
- Shorter, poised sentences
- Gentle landing

### Word Choice by Section

**Heavy Sections (E, B):** Dense, textured, active verbs
- "Sherald's raw linen holds the grisaille"
- "Brown's figures dissolve into the ground"
- "Nengudi's mesh strains without tearing"

**Medium Sections (D):** Balanced, bridging, comparative
- Contrasts between two views
- Building complexity gradually
- Introducing secondary themes

**Light Sections (A, C):** Spare, poetic, often shorter
- Single strong image
- Minimal punctuation
- Breathing room

## Thematic Transparency Across Issues

### What Changes Per Issue

| Element | Stays Constant | Changes |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| **Section order** | 1-11 always | Never |
| **Section function** | Opening → Climax → Resolution | Fixed |
| **Format slots** | 5 available (A-E) | Sequencing varies by issue |
| **Source diversity** | Enforced | 11 different sources per issue |
| **Prose tone** | Apartamento register (warm, specific, editorial) | Sonic/thematic inflection varies by Editorial Mix |
| **Theme integration** | Invisible (felt, not stated) | Density of theme varies (0-100% Theme intensity) |
| **Closing Sentence** | Must land, must be thematic | Content/word choice per issue |

### What Stays Constant

- **Section functions** (Opening Declaration through Minimal Close)
- **Prose register** (specific, warm, editorial, never academic)
- **Format assignment principles** (no consecutive repeats, natural breathing)
- **Source constraint** (no repeats except music)
- **Theme invisibility** (never stated, only felt)

## Why This Structure Works

The 11-section arc with variable format weights creates:
- **Anticipation:** Heavy opening sets stakes
- **Variety:** Format and weight changes prevent monotony
- **Pacing:** Light sections give readers visual rest
- **Climax:** Section 9 peak justifies the journey
- **Resolution:** Natural descent prevents abrupt ending
- **Thematic resonance:** Each section's visual weight supports its conceptual role

The theme becomes visible not through statement but through the *convergence* of carefully selected moments arranged in a structure that creates emotional and intellectual momentum.
