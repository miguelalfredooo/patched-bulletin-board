## Key Constraints

- Theme must be felt throughout, never explicitly stated ("we're exploring X")
- Each section contributes to the overall narrative arc
- Sources should be cited or referenced naturally within prose
- Subsections should flow together (not feel like separate articles)
- Closing section should complete the journey (visual → understanding → thinking → making → systems → material)

---

## The Prose Pattern

Each section follows a progression:
1. **VISUAL THINKING** — Start with what we see
2. **CULTURAL CONTEXT** — Then understand why it matters
3. **CRITICAL THINKING** — Then engage with the philosophy
4. **TOOLS & INNOVATION** — Then learn how to make it
5. **SYSTEMS & APPLICATION** — Then see it scale
6. **MATERIAL CULTURE STUDIES** — Then understand it materially

This creates narrative coherence across the 6 sections, ending with the material dimension.

## Process

1. Editorial Director writes Editorial Brief (with curation guidance)
2. Art Department curates 14-18 selections, organizes by 6 sections
3. Assignment Editor receives Art Department report, creates these 6 commissions
4. Assignment Editor sends commissions to Managing Editor
5. Managing Editor reads Art Department report + these commissions
6. Managing Editor writes 6 articles (one per commission)
7. Managing Editor creates 6 section visuals
8. Editorial Director validates all 6 sections + visuals
9. Bot publishes

## Example: Issue 020 — Geometry (6 Sections)

```
Section 1: VISUAL THINKING
Direction: "Start by seeing geometry visually. How do artists use pure form? 
How do painters and sculptors use composition and dimension? How do photographers 
compose with geometric precision? Show that geometry is visual thinking."

Section 2: CULTURAL CONTEXT
Direction: "What does geometry mean? Show sacred geometry across cultures. 
Then show where geometric design comes from (Bauhaus, golden ratio, mathematical principles). 
Show it's universal."

Section 3: CRITICAL THINKING
Direction: "Make the argument: Geometry is not decoration, it's philosophy. 
Designers use it because it's true. The best work starts with understanding mathematical principles."

Section 4: TOOLS & INNOVATION
Direction: "How do tools enable geometric thinking? How do algorithms use geometry? 
Show a design process using grids and proportion systems. Practical examples."

Section 5: SYSTEMS & APPLICATION
Direction: "Close with how geometric thinking scales to visual systems. 
Analyze a brand identity that uses geometric marks and grid systems. 
Show geometric thinking applied systematically."

Section 6: MATERIAL CULTURE STUDIES
Direction: "What geometric objects embody this principle? From crystals to tiles to architectural grids—
what do material manifestations of geometry teach us? How have cultures used geometric materials 
(mosaic, tessellation, sacred geometry) over time? What do hands-on, crafted geometric objects 
reveal about the principle itself?"
```
