# Art Department Report — 6-Section Template

**How The Art Department delivers curated selections organized by 6 sections.**

---

## Report Structure

```
ART DEPARTMENT REPORT — Issue [NNN]
═══════════════════════════════════

THEME: [Theme]

EDITORIAL BRIEF UNDERSTOOD:
✅ Core idea: [What's the central idea?]
✅ 6-section framework: Visual Thinking → Cultural Context → Critical Thinking → 
   Tools & Innovation → Systems & Application → Material Culture Studies
✅ Editorial insight: [What should selections teach?]

SELECTIONS CURATED: [Number] pieces organized by 6 sections

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 1: VISUAL THINKING (2-3 pieces)
How do visual disciplines approach [theme]?

SECTION 2: CULTURAL CONTEXT (2-3 pieces)
What does [theme] mean culturally and historically?

SECTION 3: CRITICAL THINKING (2-3 pieces)
Why does [theme] matter in design philosophy?

SECTION 4: TOOLS & INNOVATION (2-3 pieces)
How do makers approach [theme] with tools and process?

SECTION 5: SYSTEMS & APPLICATION (2-3 pieces)
How does [theme] scale into visual systems?

SECTION 6: MATERIAL CULTURE STUDIES (2-3 pieces) ← NEW
How does [theme] manifest as physical objects and material reality?

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EDITORIAL INSIGHT
═════════════════

These [number] selections, organized into 6 sections, reveal how [theme] is approached 
across visual disciplines, cultural contexts, design philosophy, practical tools, visual 
systems, and material reality.

[1-2 paragraph explanation of what selections collectively teach, organized by 6-section framework]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FLAGGED BUT RELEVANT: [Number] pieces
[Pieces with merit but below quality bar]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

REJECTED: [Number] pieces
[Pieces that don't fit the theme or quality standard]
```

## Key Points for 6-Section Curation

1. **Organize selections into 6 sections** (Visual, Cultural, Critical, Tools, Systems, Material Culture)
2. **2-3 pieces per section** (total 14-18)
3. **Specify subsection** for each piece (so Assignment Editor knows where it maps)
4. **Write Why/Insight** for each piece (shows your thinking)
5. **For Section 6 specifically:** Focus on physical objects, materials, manufacturing, artifacts, craft
6. **Write editorial insight paragraph** that explains what the 6-section organization reveals
7. **Preserve narrative flow** through all 6 sections

---

## Material Culture Studies — What to Look For

For Section 6, curate pieces that show:
- **Physical objects/artifacts** (ceramics, textiles, carved objects, architectural elements)
- **Material processes** (how things are made, manufactured, crafted)
- **Materiality & meaning** (what materials communicate, cultural significance)
- **Use & ritual** (how objects are used, worn, traded, ritualized)
- **Sustainability & ethics** (material sourcing, durability, lifecycle)
- **Anthropological/archaeological perspective** (what objects reveal about culture)

Examples:
- Museum exhibitions of material objects
- Craft documentation showing hands and materials
- Archaeological studies of artifacts
- Natural material studies (crystals, minerals, biological forms)
- Material engineering or textile science
- Artisan interviews about material choices
- Sustainability reports on material sources

## Bottom Line

**Curate 14-18 pieces organized by 6 sections (2-3 pieces per section).**

Section 6 (Material Culture Studies) closes the magazine by exploring how the theme manifests 
as physical objects, materials, and artifacts. This gives readers a complete understanding: 
from how they see it, to what it means culturally, to how it's made, to how it scales, 
to what it looks and feels like in the material world.
