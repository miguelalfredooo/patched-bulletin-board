# Section Art Style Guide — Bold Fill Approach

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** May 9, 2026  
**Status:** Active design direction for all section art

---

## Overview

This is the preferred style for Design By Bulletin™ section art. Use whenever applicable to create bold, balanced, confident compositions with clear visual hierarchy.

**Core Principle:** Bold fills create weight and balance. Use contrast (solid vs light) to direct visual attention.

---

## Three Primary Approaches

### 1. GEOMETRIC (Concentric)

**Best for:** Complexity, layering, inward focus, precision, structure

**Characteristics:**
- Nested squares/rectangles with inward-focusing geometry
- Symmetry on all axes (top/bottom, left/right)
- Uses solid fill (█) for weight
- Creates depth through nested layers
- Strong focal point at center

**When to use:**
- Mathematics, systems, organization
- Precision, craftsmanship, structure
- Building layers of complexity
- Inward/reflective themes

### 2. DIVIDED (Left/Right or Top/Bottom Split)

**Best for:** Duality, comparison, contrast, division, opposition

**Characteristics:**
- Hard split dividing the composition
- Two distinct fills showing equal visual weight
- Uses solid (█) vs light (░) for contrast
- Optional divider line (║ or ─) marking the seam
- Both sides carry equal importance

### 3. CENTER FOCUS (Radiating/Nested with Field)

**Best for:** Emergence, presence, emphasis, stillness, presence in void

**Characteristics:**
- Nested shape centered with radiating hierarchy
- Surrounded by texture field (░) that contrasts with center (█)
- Creates strong focal point with breathing room
- Hierarchy from dense center to light edges
- Uses white space effectively

## Fill Characters & Contrast

### Solid Fills (Visual Weight)

- `█` — Full solid block (darkest, most weight)
- `▓` — Dark shade (heavy)
- `▒` — Medium shade (medium weight)
- `░` — Light shade (subtle weight)

### Dividers (Optional)

- `║` — Vertical divider (left/right splits)
- `─` — Horizontal divider (top/bottom splits)
- `═` — Double line (stronger division)

### Contrast Principle

Strong contrast = strong visual impact
- `█` (solid) vs `░` (light) = maximum contrast
- Use two adjacent fill levels for hierarchy
- Never use same fill twice (no visual distinction)

---

## Technical Specs (Always)

- **Width:** 34 characters FIXED (every line padded)
- **Height:** Max 15 lines (can be less)
- **Padding:** All lines exactly 34 chars (spaces at end)
- **ASCII:** Pure ASCII only (box-drawing + fills)

## Design Checklist (Before Delivery)

- [ ] Approach chosen: Geometric / Divided / Center Focus
- [ ] Bold fills used confidently (█ and ░ contrast)
- [ ] Composition is balanced (symmetry or equal weight)
- [ ] Visual hierarchy is clear (focal point present)
- [ ] Every line = exactly 34 characters
- [ ] Max 15 lines (can be less)
- [ ] No placeholder text (actual visual only)
- [ ] Shapes are bold and confident (not timid)
- [ ] Theme integrated (visual reinforces section topic)

## Examples by Theme

### For Rhythm/Motion/Energy
**Best approach:** Geometric (nested pattern) or Divided (showing forward motion)

### For Stillness/Void/Emptiness
**Best approach:** Center Focus (single element with breathing room)

### For Duality/Contrast/Choice
**Best approach:** Divided (equal weight left/right or top/bottom)

### For Precision/Systems/Order
**Best approach:** Geometric (nested, symmetrical)

### For Emergence/Focus/Presence
**Best approach:** Center Focus (nested center with field)

---

## When NOT to Use Bold Fill Style

- Organic, flowing, gestural themes (use flowing lines instead)
- Complexity that needs detail (use finer patterns)
- Asymmetrical/chaotic concepts (use scattered approach)
- Literal representation (e.g., actual objects)

*Note: Always consider if bold fill approach serves the section. If a different style better reinforces the theme, use that.*

---

## Implementation Notes

**For Design Department:**
- This is a preferred style, not a requirement
- Use whenever bold, balanced, geometric expression fits the theme
- Combine with other techniques for variety across 11 sections
- Always validate that style matches section concept

**For Editorial:**
- Verify that chosen style reinforces section thesis
- Check that visual weight and balance are confident (not timid)
- Ensure fills are used clearly (not muddled)
