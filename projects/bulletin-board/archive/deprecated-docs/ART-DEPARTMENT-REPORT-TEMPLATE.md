## Key Principles for This Report

### Organization
- **Organize by narrative angle**, not by section or date
- **Group related selections** so they speak to each other
- **Build arc within each angle** (simple to complex, or foundation to application)

### Annotation (Why / Insight / Connection)
- **Why** — What is this work? What does it do? (1-2 sentences, concrete)
- **Insight** — Why does it matter for this theme? (1-2 sentences, meaning)
- **Connection** — How does it relate to other pieces? (1 sentence, bridges)

### Editorial Insight Paragraph
- **Name the dimensions** (the 2-3 angles you explored)
- **Explain connections** (how angles relate to each other)
- **State the insight** (what does this teach about the theme?)
- **Make it poetic** (language should reflect the theme's importance)

### Quality Standards
- All PASS selections should meet: ⭐⭐ minimum (4 of 5 dimensions)
- Selection is curated (chosen with taste), not algorithmic (highest scores)
- Flagged pieces show you evaluated carefully (merit but not quite bar)
- Rejected pieces show you defended the theme (no weak inclusions)

## What Assignment Editor Needs

When you submit this report, Assignment Editor will:
1. **Read your selections** (understand what you chose)
2. **Read your annotations** (understand why each matters)
3. **Read your insight paragraph** (understand what theme means)
4. **Map pieces to sections** (assign to the 11 sections)
5. **Identify gaps** (see what needs commissioning)
6. **Commission custom pieces** (fill gaps you flagged)

Your annotations are how Assignment Editor understands your thinking.
Your insight paragraph is how they'll write good commission briefs.
Your narrative arc is how they'll keep the story intact.

Make it clear. Make it compelling. Make it useful.
