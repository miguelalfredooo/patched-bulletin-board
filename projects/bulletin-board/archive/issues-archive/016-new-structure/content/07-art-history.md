**Art History**

The UFO has a design history. In the 1950s, saucers were sleek, chrome, confident—reflecting post-war technological optimism. In the 1970s, designs became organic, less geometric, as if reflecting anxiety about technology replacing nature. Modern UFO descriptions shift toward the ambiguous, hard-to-visualize: craft that don't follow Euclidean geometry. These morphological changes aren't random. Design becomes forensics: by studying what we draw in the sky, we learn what we're afraid of, what we desire.
