The antenna receives. It's pointed at something we can't see. It asks a question it doesn't know how to answer. But culturally, it's more: a gesture toward the unknown, a tool of faith disguised as engineering. The antenna assumes something is broadcasting, that the universe isn't silent. On rooftops, it's practical and wistful: a monument to the possibility of connection. The antenna unites all these themes: space exploration, UFO signals, electronic music as antenna, the spirit world as frequency.
