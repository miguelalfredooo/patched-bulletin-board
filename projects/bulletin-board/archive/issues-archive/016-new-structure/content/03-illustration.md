Boards of Canada don't make music—they make transmissions from a future that might not exist. Analog tape loops, warm synthesizers, field recordings combined to create the uncanny. Their method mimics memory itself: a 1980s synthesizer playing a melody from the 1960s, over recordings from nowhere. Electronic music becomes spiritual technology when it cracks the boundaries between past and present.
