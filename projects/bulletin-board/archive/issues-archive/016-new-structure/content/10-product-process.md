**Product & Process**

Electronic music requires active listening. The synthesizer's textures demand attention: a sound hovering at the edge of perception, a delay asking a question, reverb mimicking spaces between things. Creating conditions for this listening requires intention: a quiet room, speakers positioned to create space, time without interruption. This is radical in a culture obsessed with content and convenience. When you sit down to listen to Boards of Canada or Autechre—really listen—you're practicing resistance. This sound is worth the time it takes to truly hear it.
