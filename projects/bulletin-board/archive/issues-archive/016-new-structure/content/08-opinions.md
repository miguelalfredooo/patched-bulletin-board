**Opinions**

Most astronomers know better than to investigate the anomalous. The pressure toward conventional explanations is immense. But there are researchers who ask what remains unexplained: objects that don't fit known physics, signals that don't match any catalogued source. They work defensively—aware that too much curiosity makes you an outsider. What they've learned: the universe is stranger than we admit. There's a category of legitimate scientific bewilderment we're trained to ignore.
