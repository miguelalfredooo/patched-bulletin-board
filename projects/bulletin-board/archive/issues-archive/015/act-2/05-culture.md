**Culture**

The Art Newspaper's recent essay names something that's been accumulating: AI-generated political imagery is now the visual language of multiple world governments — Trump's divine iconography, IRGC animated Lego propaganda, protest graphics assembled in minutes — and the effect is a loss of reverberation, images that provoke without resonating, that circulate without landing. The argument is not that speed is wrong but that slop has no frequency; it cannot carry meaning across the gap between the maker and the receiver.
