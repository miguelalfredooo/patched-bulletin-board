**Opinions**

Thom Yorke and Stanley Donwood — who have designed every Radiohead visual since 1994 — opened a joint exhibition in Venice this week, and the question their collaboration poses is the same one it's always posed: what survives the gap between the music and the image, between intention and reception, between making and being made? Their answer, after thirty years, seems to be: the frequency. The specific note the collaborators share. Not the image, not the sound — the resonance between them.
