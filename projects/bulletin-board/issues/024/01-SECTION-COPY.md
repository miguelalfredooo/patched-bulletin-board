# Art

There's a moment when a shape stops being decoration and starts being language. Icons do this quietly — a circle, an arrow, a eye rendered flat — and somewhere in the viewer, something clicks. Artists have always understood this. Magritte's pipe is a lesson in the gap between symbol and thing. Barbara Kruger's bold sans-serif demands are icons in the purest sense: high-contrast, immediate, impossible to misread. When art works at the level of the icon, it bypasses argument and goes straight to recognition. That's not a shortcut. That's precision.
