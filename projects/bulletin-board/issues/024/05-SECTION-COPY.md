# Culture

Icons are cultural compression. The red cross, the crescent, the Star of David — each encodes centuries of meaning into a geometry that crosses languages and borders. Emojis are the newest members of this lineage, not a degraded form of communication but an evolved one. A yellow circle with two dots and a curved line can express warmth, sarcasm, or grief depending on context. That's not imprecision — that's how all symbol systems work. Culture loads meaning onto form over time, and form carries it forward. The icon is how civilizations remember what they believe.
