Historians see movement as transformation. The Renaissance, Art Deco, the avant-garde—each was a movement because it moved cultures forward. But movement itself, as a subject, reveals how human understanding has evolved. Futurism celebrated speed. Jackson Pollock made gesture visible on canvas. Contemporary artists investigate slowness as radical. We can see in the history of art how our relationship with motion—to rhythm, to urgency, to presence—has fundamentally shifted.
