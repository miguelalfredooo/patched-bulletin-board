Every object is the result of movement: the hand guiding the tool, the process unfurling over time, the craft of making. When we understand how something moves from concept to completion, we honor the labor that shapes it. A chair is not just form—it's the choreography of a designer, the skill of makers, the patience of the process. To know how something was made is to know the movement that created it. This knowledge changes how we value what we hold.
