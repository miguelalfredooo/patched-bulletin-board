**Sculpture**

Gabrielle Goliath's *Elegy* — commissioned for the South African pavilion, rerouted to a Venice church after the pavilion's cancellation — is a sound installation in which voices perform mourning across multiple channels, grief shaped into frequency rather than image. The space becomes the instrument: stone walls, candlelit air, the particular reverberation of a place built for sound to last longer than usual.
