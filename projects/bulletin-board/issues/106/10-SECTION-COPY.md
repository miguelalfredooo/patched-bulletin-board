**Product & Process — The Cost of Precision**

Typographer Matthew Carter spent six months refining a single font family. The difference between version one and version six? Width relationships that most users would never consciously register. But they'd feel it—the text would just sit better on the page. He spent time on something invisible. Not because he had to. Because it was wrong without it. Detail work looks simple because the labor hides. What you don't see is the revision, the constraint, the "no that's not right yet" repeated fifty times. That hidden work is what makes clarity possible.
