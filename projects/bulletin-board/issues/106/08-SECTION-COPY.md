**Opinions — The Case for Slowness**

In a world of speed, detail is an act of resistance. Not romanticism. Practicality. The typographer who spends three hours on a single word-space doesn't do it to slow production—they do it because that space determines whether the text breathes or suffocates. The difference costs nothing. But it requires paying attention. We live in a time that monetizes attention-deficit. Detail requires you do the opposite: look at one thing until it becomes clear. That's not nostalgia. That's the most contemporary choice possible.
