**Painting — Scale Against Itself**

Sarah Sze builds paintings where a loose gesture contains seventeen layers of precision underneath. You see a smear of color from across the room. Move closer: it's made of painted threads. Closer still: each thread is a landscape. The scale collapses into itself. Your eye has to decide which distance is real. That collapse—the moment you realize both scales exist simultaneously—is what makes the work breathe. Detail doesn't finish a painting. It destabilizes it.
