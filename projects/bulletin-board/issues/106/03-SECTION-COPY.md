**Illustration — The Hidden Diagram**

Carson Ellis draws worlds so crowded they seem chaotic at first glance. Then you start tracking a thread—a single figure across five pages, a pattern that repeats with one element different each time. The detail becomes a language. It's not ornament. It's how she tells you to pay attention. Illustration that works this way trusts readers to do work, to follow, to notice. That trust built into visual complexity—that's detail as narrative intelligence.
