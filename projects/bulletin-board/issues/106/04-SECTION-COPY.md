**Sculpture — The Detail Nobody Sees**

On the back of Anish Kapoor's sculptures—the side no one photographs, the part only the installer will ever touch—there are marks, refinements, adjustments. These details exist for rightness, not recognition. They're the maker's private conversation with the material. This is a different kind of precision: not about being seen, but about knowing the thing is right even in its hidden dimensions. The detail you choose when no one will verify your choice. That's intention at its purest.
