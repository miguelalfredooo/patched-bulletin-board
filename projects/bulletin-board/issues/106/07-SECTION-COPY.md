**Art History — The Swing of the Pendulum**

Renaissance painters spent years learning to render each eyelash. Minimalists rejected all that, calling it distraction. Contemporary art brings obsessive detail back—but not the same kind. The difference: Renaissance detail proved mastery. Contemporary detail proves observation. When a culture decides detail is irrelevant (Modernism's great no), it says: "Only the essential shape matters." When it comes back (Maximalism's yes), it says: "Actually, the small things were always the point." The pendulum reveals what each era thinks knowledge is.
