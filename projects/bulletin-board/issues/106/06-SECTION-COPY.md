**Photography — The Visible Invisible**

Photographer Richard Avedon lit his portraits so precisely that the photograph revealed pores your eye couldn't see in real life. The camera didn't simplify—it revealed. Same with close-up photography: the detail exists in nature; the camera just gives us permission to stare long enough to actually notice it. Photography is the technology that said: "Yes, that microscopic texture matters. Yes, that dust particle is beautiful. Your instinct to look closer is correct." The camera validates attention.
