**Culture — The Learned Eye**

Japanese woodworkers spend years understanding wood grain. Not learning *about* it, but actually perceiving it—the way it moves, breathes, resists. This isn't skill. This is culture training you to see. Oenology did the same thing. Bird-watching did the same thing. These are all systems where a community agrees that noticing detail matters enough to spend time learning. The precision isn't natural. It's taught. Which means any culture that stops teaching attention loses the ability to see what's real.
