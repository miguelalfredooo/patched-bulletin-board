# Material Culture Studies

Craft is always about restraint. A potter learns negative space by creating vessels—the clay removed is as intentional as the clay placed. Japanese ceramics teach that a crack or void is not damage but character. The absence becomes the point.

In typography, the space between letters (kerning) is where craft lives. It's invisible until it's wrong. Master typefaces have breathing room built into their DNA. The silence between characters is tuned like a musical interval.

Contemporary makers understand: what you choose not to make is as powerful as what you do. In this paradox, emptiness becomes the most valuable material.
