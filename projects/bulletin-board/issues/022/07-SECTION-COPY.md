**Motion Through Emptiness**

Negative space isn't just visual—it's temporal. Animation lives in the pause. A film director knows: the space between frames creates movement. The silence between notes creates rhythm. A dancer's empty stage is more powerful than a stage crowded with props.

Contemporary motion design has learned this truth: the fastest way to show movement is to show stillness interrupted. A figure against vastness. A gesture surrounded by void. The eye moves faster through empty space, so the design feels lighter, more alive.

In interaction design, this translates to refinement. Loading states that breathe. Transitions that float through whitespace. The emptiness itself moves you forward. Restraint in motion becomes speed.
