**The Courage to Leave It Alone**

Negative space requires something contemporary culture rarely tolerates: patience. A designer with an empty page faces a question: what if nothing is the answer? Most systems reward filling space. More content. More features. More visibility.

The designers we respect are the ones who resisted. They trusted emptiness. They knew that subtraction is harder than addition, that saying no to everything except the essential takes more courage than saying yes to everything.

This is where design becomes philosophy. Negative space is a stance toward sufficiency, toward enough, toward the belief that a life well-lived has breathing room. Every constraint we design into a space—every margin, every pause—is an argument about what matters. The things we choose not to include define what remains.
