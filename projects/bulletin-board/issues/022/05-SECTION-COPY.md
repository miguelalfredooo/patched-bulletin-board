# Systems & Application

Apple's products are museums of negative space. The iPhone's screen is mostly black. An Apple Watch face is mostly void. This isn't laziness—it's restraint at scale.

In UI design, white space isn't wasted screen real estate. It's oxygen for the interface. It lets the user breathe. It makes the tap targets obvious. It says "this matters" by surrounding it with nothing.

From product design to web to environmental space, negative space has become the primary tool of contemporary practice. What you exclude defines what remains.
