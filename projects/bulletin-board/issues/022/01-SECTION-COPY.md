# Visual Thinking

Negative space is the foundation of visual perception. When we see form, we're actually seeing the relationship between object and void. Gestalt psychology revealed this truth decades ago: the mind completes what the eye doesn't fully show.

In architecture, a brutalist concrete wall gains its power from the scale of emptiness around it. In typography, the space between letters is as important as the letters themselves. Remove the silence and the rhythm collapses.

This is where design clarity lives. Not in what you add, but in what you refuse to fill.
