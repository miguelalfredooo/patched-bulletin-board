**The Geometry of Breathing**

Designers use golden ratio, rule of thirds, and proportional systems not to make designs look good, but to make them feel breathable. A margin too small and the page suffocates. A margin just right and everything expands.

This is empirical. Eye-tracking studies show where viewers rest. Heatmaps show where the eye naturally pauses. These aren't aesthetic observations—they're physiological facts. The human eye needs landing space to relax.

Contemporary digital design is learning this the hard way. Mobile-first design with generous margins outperforms cramped layouts in every metric—engagement, comprehension, satisfaction. The emptiness isn't wasted space. It's infrastructure for attention.
