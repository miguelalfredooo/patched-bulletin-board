**Silence in Sound Design**

The best sound designers know that silence is a sound. A ringing phone in a quiet room means emergency. Silence in a crowded concert hall means intimacy. The absence of noise communicates just as loudly as noise itself.

In UI design, audio has become sparse and careful. A single chime. A brief vibration. Long stretches of nothing. Every sound is now precious because attention is scarce. Restraint in audio reflects restraint everywhere else—we're all learning to value emptiness, even in domains that once obsessed over filling every moment.

Contemporary composers understand: the notes that aren't played are as important as the notes that are. Silence is a compositional tool, not a failure of the orchestra.
