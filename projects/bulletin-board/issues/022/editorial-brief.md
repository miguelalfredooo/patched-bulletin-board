## Theme

Negative space is not emptiness—it's presence through absence. The breathing room around objects, the silence between notes, the whitespace that defines form. In design, negative space is where clarity lives. It's the most powerful tool a designer has, and it's invisible. This issue explores how designers think about what they *don't* include.

## Editorial Insight

The best designers are subtractive, not additive. They know that removing is harder than adding. Negative space is restraint. It's confidence. It's saying "this is enough" and trusting the viewer to complete the picture. When a design breathes, it communicates.
