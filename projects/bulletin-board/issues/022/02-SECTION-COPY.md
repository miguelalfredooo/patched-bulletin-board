# Cultural Context

Different cultures have different relationships with emptiness. In the West, white space is efficiency—the page is real estate, and empty means wasted. In the East, emptiness is a presence. It breathes. It invites.

Zen aesthetics taught that what you remove is as important as what remains. A Japanese garden is 80% stone and sky. A Swiss poster from 1960 is mostly blank paper. Both are saying the same thing: trust the void.

This isn't about poverty or minimalism as style. It's about confidence. It's about knowing when you're done.
