## Diaspora Consciousness — Between Home and the World

There is a specific moment in diaspora consciousness when you realize the world wants what you are. When you're living your split-ness—rooted in one place, shaped by another—and suddenly the place that shaped you starts wanting what the place you left behind has always known.

When Puerto Rican creators deepen instead of soften, when chefs protect convivencia as philosophy, when designers claim their aesthetic as rooted not trendy, when drummers trace lineage back to Africa—they're teaching diaspora consciousness. Not how to leave home. How to practice home. How to keep what matters alive across distance and time.

When the world reaches for Puerto Rico, it reaches for this: the knowledge of how to live intentionally. Home is a practice. And that practice travels.
