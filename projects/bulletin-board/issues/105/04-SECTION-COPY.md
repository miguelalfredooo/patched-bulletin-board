**Sculpture — Geometry Made Visible**

A sculptor must engineer meaning. The work has to *stand*—not just conceptually but physically, against gravity, through time. This is where structure stops being philosophy and becomes material reality. Richard Serra's steel arcs don't whisper about balance; they *are* balance, the internal geometry revealed as emotional experience. When you feel the weight of metal suspended between precision and chaos, you're feeling structure made visible. The skeleton becomes the sculpture. The engineering is the art.
