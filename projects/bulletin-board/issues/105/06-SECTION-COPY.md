**Photography — The Frame as Active Geometry**

The rectangle isn't neutral. It *organizes* seeing. A photographer like Diane Arbus used the frame not as transparent window but as active structure—centered subjects, formal distance, the grid as composition made visible. Her photographs feel architectural because the frame is doing the work. Look at contemporary work using systematic sequences, gridded presentations, structured series: the photograph becomes proof that constraint *shapes* vision. The frame isn't where the subject lives; it's what makes the subject visible at all.
