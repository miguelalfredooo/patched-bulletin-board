**Art — The Grid as Liberation**

Vera Molnár didn't see grids as prison. She saw them as permission. In the 1960s, when she began writing algorithms to generate compositions—strict geometric systems, regulated intervals, mathematical precision—her work exploded into endless variation. The constraint *was* the freedom. Each grid contained infinite possibility. She taught us that formal rules don't compress imagination; they focus it. The artist working inside a system makes choices the drifting artist never gets to make. Clarity through structure.
