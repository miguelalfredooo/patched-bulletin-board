**Painting — The Invisible Armature**

A painter's composition isn't accident. Under every work lives a skeleton—armature lines, proportional relationships, the rule of thirds operating whether the painter names it or not. Agnes Martin spent decades painting grids, simple lines on canvas, and discovered something: precision doesn't flatten emotion; it *clarifies* it. Each grid variation became an instrument for tenderness, for presence. The structure held the feeling in place. This is how painting works at its deepest. The architecture doesn't show. The life inside it does.
