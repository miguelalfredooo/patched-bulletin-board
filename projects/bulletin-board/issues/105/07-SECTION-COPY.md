**Art History — The System as Artwork**

Sol LeWitt didn't make objects; he made instructions. A "Wall Drawing" is a set of rules—draw lines, follow this pattern, repeat until the wall is full. Different hands execute it, infinite variations emerge, yet the *structure* remains identical. He proved the radical idea: the system itself is the artwork. Minimalism, Constructivism, Systems Art—entire movements were built on this principle. The constraint, the rule, the formal logic *becomes* content. This shifted what art could be: from object to process, from fixed to generative.
