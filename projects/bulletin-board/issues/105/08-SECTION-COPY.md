**Opinions — The Courage to Work Inside Constraints**

We're sold the myth: freedom means no rules, expression means no limits. But look at every maker who created something that lasted. They worked *inside* brutal constraints—canvas size, pigment chemistry, the physics of their medium. The miracle isn't that they ignored constraints; it's that they became fluent inside them, the way a poet masters meter and then breaks it *meaningfully*. The hard truth: constraint requires more creativity, not less. It's harder to say something profound in a sonnet than in rambling prose. The courage isn't breaking free; it's committing deeply to a form.
