**Illustration — The Modular World**

Studio Ghibli doesn't animate chaos. They animated worlds through consistent systems—character proportions, movement libraries, color palettes that repeat until they feel like law. Each frame builds from identical foundations. This modularity is what lets a small team create something vast and coherent. Modern illustrators build character design systems, component libraries, proportional grids that scale across projects. The system is invisible to the viewer but absolute in the maker's hands. Structure enables coherence at scale without losing voice.
