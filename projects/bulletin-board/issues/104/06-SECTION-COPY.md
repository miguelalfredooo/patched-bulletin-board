**Photography — Documentary of Disappearance**

Photographers have a peculiar power: they capture exactly what is about to vanish. Paul Graham documenting the American mall before its collapse. Sohei Natsuka photographing disappearing Japanese villages with formal precision. Diane Arbus knew her subjects would die. The photograph isn't a record of permanence—it's a record of loss. The aesthetic becomes elegiac whether the subject is monumental or mundane. A store closing. A street renamed. A landscape swallowed by water. The photographer's task isn't to resist disappearance but to witness it clearly. To say: this was here. This mattered. Now it's gone. The image carries the weight of that transformation.
