**Visual & Brand — Logo Archaeology**

Every rebrand is a small burial. Companies erase their histories in the name of freshness, smoothing away character to become generic. Mozilla's evolution is a chronicle of erosion—each iteration slightly flatter, slightly safer. But some brands wear their weathering visibly. Mastercard's iconic rings have survived decades of minor shifts because they bent with time. The oldest logos are archaeological objects, showing us what felt important enough to keep when everything else could be discarded. A brand that erodes authentically shows its age. A brand that fights erosion hardens into irrelevance. Logo archaeology isn't nostalgia. It's evidence: what did they protect? What did they let go? The history visible in a mark tells you what actually mattered.
