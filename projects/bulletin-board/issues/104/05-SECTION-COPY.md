**Culture — Linguistic Erosion**

Regional accents flatten across generations. Dialects collapse into standard speech. The precise vocabulary of a craft erodes as the craft itself vanishes. But the opposite also happens: subcultures carve away at the dominant language, eroding its authority through slang, code-switching, and intentional incomprehensibility. The linguistic front line is where meaning wears down and gets rebuilt. A word that was sacred becomes neutral becomes invisible. A phrase that was fringe becomes vernacular. Linguistic erosion isn't passive—it's a form of cultural wrestling. Who controls what words mean? The answer: whoever wears them down fastest. The language that survives is the one that's been tested.
