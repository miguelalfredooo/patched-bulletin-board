**Sculpture — Material Giving Way**

Richard Serra's steel plates rust intentionally, their oxidation documented as part of the work. In the gardens of Storm King, his sculptures accumulate patina. Andy Goldsworthy makes pieces from ice and leaves knowing they'll dissolve before the day ends. The Japanese stone gardens of Kyoto have weathered into shapes that might not be human-made anymore. These sculptors don't fight entropy—they collaborate with it. The piece changes meaning every season. What was sharp becomes soft, what was whole becomes partial. The marble that took a year to carve yields to frost and rain, and the artist watches this with something like reverence. Material doesn't betray intention. It completes it.
