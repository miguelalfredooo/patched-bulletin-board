**Product & Process — Planned Obsolescence / Earned Durability**

Some products are designed to erode quickly. Others are built to resist. The strategies diverge completely: planned obsolescence versus modularity, disposability versus repairability. A phone meant to last three years versus a hand tool designed to be sharpened across generations. The process reveals the philosophy. Companies that embrace erosion as inevitable often choose seriality—make something good, release it, move on. Companies that resist erosion invest in repairability, in parts that can be replaced, in objects that improve with use. Japanese toolmakers still sharpen blades passed down from ancestors. Patagonia redesigns jackets for mending. The process of making something durable is the opposite of the process of making something disposable.
