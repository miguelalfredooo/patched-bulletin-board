**Art History — The Canon Softening**

The artists you learned in school have eroded into footnotes. The certainties your teachers taught have weathered under new scholarship. Hilma af Klint, ignored for decades, is now essential. The old master narrative—linear, triumphant, European—has worn thin under counternarrative and recontextualization. What we called universal was provincial. What we called timeless was temporary. The canon isn't being preserved—it's being recycled. Artists emerge from obscurity not because history changed, but because we finally stopped filtering through a worn-out lens. Art history's erosion isn't loss. It's revelation. The softer the old authority becomes, the sharper new clarity gets.
