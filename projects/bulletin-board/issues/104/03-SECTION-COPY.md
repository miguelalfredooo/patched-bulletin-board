**Illustration — Precision Dissolving**

Philip Guston's late work moved from meticulous abstraction toward crude, almost childlike scrawl. Or reverse the angle: Katsutaka Taira builds impossibly detailed cityscapes, then deliberately obscures them in shadow and atmospheric haze. Precision is interesting only when it's tested against disappearance. There's a particular genre of illustration that documents systems breaking apart—grids losing their geometry, networks becoming tangles, perfect hierarchies collapsing into static. The illustrator's eye moves from "how much detail can I pack in?" to "what matters when everything dissolves?" The answer: usually very little.
