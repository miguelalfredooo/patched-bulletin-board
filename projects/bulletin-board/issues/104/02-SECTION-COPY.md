**Painting — Color Wearing Away**

Hilma af Klint painted in sequences, watching color shift and fade across decades. Not restoration. The paint oxidizes, pigments chemically decline, and the artist accepts this. David Hockney's swimming pools bleach into near-whiteness through deliberate technical choice. Agnes Martin's color grids fade so gradually you wonder if your eyes are failing. These paintings don't resist their own weathering—they embrace it. The fading becomes part of the statement. Dust settles. Color surrenders. What emerges is honesty: not what the artist demanded the canvas show, but what time allows it to reveal.
