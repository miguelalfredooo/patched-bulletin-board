**Art — Erosion of Gesture**

When Anselm Kiefer layered ash, straw, and lead into canvas, he was making time visible. The mark dissolves not through careless ruin but through deliberate forgetting. Agnes Martin spent decades drawing nearly invisible lines, letting the viewer's eye do the erosion. Or take Cy Twombly's scribbles—gesture made urgent, then allowed to weather into barely-legible traces. These artists understood something: the work isn't finished when intention is clear. It's finished when the hand disappears. What remains is the absence.
