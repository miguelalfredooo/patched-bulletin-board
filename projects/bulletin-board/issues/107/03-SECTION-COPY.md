**Illustration — Genealogy Visible**

Every Puerto Rican dish is a three-way conversation frozen in time. Yuca and yams carry Taíno memory—names, growing methods, the way they respond to island soil. Okra, rice techniques, the frying method: African diaspora anchored in the bodies of cooks whose ancestors survived the middle passage. Sofrito, stews, the architecture of the plate: Spanish colonialism, layered deliberately or unconsciously into every meal. An illustrated genealogy makes this visible. Map the lineage—show how mofongo holds three continents in its composition. Make visible the hidden conversation. The reader sees the dish differently once they understand it's not just food; it's a three-way dialogue, a refusal to be simplified, an insistence on complexity in the face of erasure.
