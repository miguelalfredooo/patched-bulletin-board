**Painting — Color as Flavor Memory**

The deep burgundy of mofongo. The saffron gold of arroz con pollo. The charred black of tostones against white ceramic. Puerto Rican cuisine has a chromatic language—one that lives in the body before words. Paint these colors and they become memory. Not literal depictions of food, but sensory translations: the moment the oil hits the sofrito, the release of garlic and cilantro, the heat rising. The painter doesn't show the dish; they show the *sensation* of eating it. These are paintings for people who've tasted what they're seeing. Color becomes a bridge between inheritance and body, between what was tasted in childhood and what's being tasted now.
