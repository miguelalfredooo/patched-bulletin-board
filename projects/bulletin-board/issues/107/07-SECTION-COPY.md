**Art History — Who Names the Cuisine**

Colonial archives recorded Puerto Rican food through outside eyes—anthropologists, missionaries, imperial record-keepers—always with the bias of empire. These documents matter, but they're not the story. The real story is contemporary: Puerto Rican writers reclaiming the authority to document their own food, to name it, to define it. This is decolonization—taking back the right to narrate. A cookbook written by a Puerto Rican cook carries different weight than one by a visiting anthropologist. The first has earned authority; the second has only imperial credibility. The shift from being observed to being the observer—that's the move that matters. Food history, like all history, is power.
