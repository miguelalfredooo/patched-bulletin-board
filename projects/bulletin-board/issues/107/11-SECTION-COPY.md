**Visual & Brand — The Aesthetic of Everyday Feast**

Puerto Rican visual culture—from kitchen walls to restaurant signage to home altars—encodes philosophy. Color is abundance. Arrangement is care. A kitchen wall painted bright yellow isn't trend; it's a statement about joy being refusal. The way a table is set communicates values that words would require paragraphs to explain. These visual decisions are intelligent. Minimalism is a luxury for people who don't have to squeeze meaning into every corner. Puerto Rican visual culture maximizes: color, texture, abundance—all saying the same thing: *we survive, we celebrate, we refuse diminishment*. These choices are design. They carry meaning. They're not decoration. They're architecture of resistance made visible.
