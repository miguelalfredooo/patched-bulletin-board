**Opinions — Against Nostalgia, For Continuity**

The myth goes: abuela knew the real way; everything now is degraded. This is a trap. Puerto Rican cuisine is a *living system*, changing daily. A chef in San Juan cooks differently than one in New York; both cook differently than a home cook in Orlando. Climate, immigration, economic pressure—all reshape the food. The food survives precisely *because* it changes. Continuity isn't about perfection or authenticity; it's about intelligence adapting under pressure. When we trap Puerto Rican food in nostalgia, we rob it of agency and make it a relic. It's actually a system so robust it survives colonialism, diaspora, poverty, and feeds people with dignity. The food isn't dying. It's alive.
