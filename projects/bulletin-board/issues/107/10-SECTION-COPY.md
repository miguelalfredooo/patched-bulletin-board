**Product & Process — From Seed to Table**

Take mofongo backward: from the finished plate to the distributor, to the farmer growing plantain. Some plantains are industrial—bred for shipping, treated with pesticides, designed for transport. Others are heirloom—varieties selected over centuries for flavor and soil compatibility. Colonial agricultural patterns prioritized export crops over food security; that legacy shapes what grows now. Some ingredients survive because families refused to switch. Others vanished. The supply chain isn't neutral infrastructure—it's choices about what survives, what's valued, who profits. Understanding the supply chain of a single meal means understanding colonialism, economics, environmental collapse, and resistance. When a cook chooses heirloom plantain, they're reading the entire system and saying: this one survives.
