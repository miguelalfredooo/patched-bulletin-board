**Design & AI Tools — Mapping Flavor Algorithmically**

What does a machine learn when trained on sofrito? Can code understand terroir? When we model Puerto Rican cuisine as data—flavor compounds, temperature curves, timing sequences—what becomes visible and what vanishes? Experimental work exploring food through visualization or AI can illuminate if it asks questions instead of providing answers. What does an algorithm see that a cook doesn't? What does a cook know that data can't capture? Some tools augment human knowledge—mapping what we understood into new visibility. Others flatten complexity into quantity, and that loss matters. The critical work isn't in the tool; it's asking: who benefits from ancestral knowledge becoming algorithmic? What gets lost?
