**Photography — The Table as Ceremony**

The photograph captures the moment before eating—the arrangement not yet disturbed, the hands at rest, the faces ready. Not candid. Intentional. In Puerto Rican culture, the ordinary meal is a ceremony. The table is set with the same care a museum gives a painting. Close-ups show specific dishes at completion: the steam rising from rice and beans, the crust on a tostón, the dark gloss of mofongo. Wide shots capture the table's ecology—the worn ceramic, the hands of the person about to eat, the way the light hits. The photography should convey simultaneity: the everyday and the sacred exist in the same moment, on the same plate. These aren't precious images. They're documents of how a culture survives by treating the ordinary as ceremonial, and the ceremonial as simply how we live.
