**Art — Kitchens as Laboratories**

A sofrito doesn't happen. It's engineered. Layer sofrito—garlic, onion, pepper—until the chemistry shifts. Each ingredient breaks down on its own timeline; the cook reads the kitchen's temperature, the pan's color, adjusts. This is material knowledge, the same precision a sculptor brings to stone. In Puerto Rican kitchens, the work is deliberate: mofongo's texture depends on hand-pressure and timing; the composition of a plate tells the story of who cooked it, where they learned, what they refuse to rush. The hands-on process is art-making. The kitchen is a laboratory where technique becomes inseparable from identity.
