**Movement Through Scatter: Navigation in Distributed Space**

Walking through a scattered landscape is different from walking through a grid. The grid offers efficiency—clear paths, predictable routes. Scatter offers exploration—possibility of unexpected discovery, serendipity, emerging paths.

Contemporary user experience struggles with this paradox. We want clear paths (efficiency) but also exploration (engagement). The solution is scatter with subtle guidance. Scattered elements that create natural flows. Information scattered in ways that reward wandering while still allowing those who want to hurry to cut through.

This mirrors how we navigate physical cities. The grid of Manhattan enables speed but punishes exploration. The scattered medieval city rewards wandering but frustrates efficiency. The best designs scatter information while laying invisible trails—paths that feel natural and unforced but actually guide you toward your destination.
