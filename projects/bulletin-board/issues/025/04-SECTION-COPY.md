**Gathering at the Center: When Scatter Points Toward Unity**

A scattered field of elements feels random until a single point anchors them. A sun surrounded by planets. A fire surrounded by people. A brand mark surrounded by supporting imagery. The scatter gains meaning through the point it orbits.

This is compositional gravity. Without a center, scatter is noise. With a center, scatter becomes a system. The eye enters at the center and radiates outward, or enters from the periphery and is drawn inward. Gravity makes scatter coherent.

Contemporary design uses this principle everywhere: hero images surrounded by distributed content. A speaker surrounded by scattered audience. A product surrounded by scattered lifestyle imagery. The center doesn't need to be large or obvious—just present enough to hold the scattered elements in its field.
