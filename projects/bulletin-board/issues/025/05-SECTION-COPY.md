**Scale and Distance: The Scattered Perspective**

In visual representation, scatter reveals depth. Near and far. Small and large. A scattered composition has perspective built into it—objects at different distances from the viewer, scattered across planes rather than flattened into a plane.

Renaissance painters learned this: scatter objects at different depths to create the illusion of space. Modern designers use the same tool. Scattered elements create distance. Items overlap, suggesting near and far. The scatter becomes a map of depth.

This is why particle systems in digital design feel so alive. They exist in three dimensions. They scatter through space, creating the sensation of depth even on a flat screen. The scatter isn't just visual interest—it's spatial information. It tells you where you are and how far you can see.
