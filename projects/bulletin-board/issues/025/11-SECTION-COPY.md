**The Beauty of Dispersal: Embracing Scatter as a Way of Being**

We want to organize everything. To centralize, simplify, control. But the universe teaches a different lesson: scatter is not failure. It is the primary state of matter. Solidity is the rare condition. Dispersal is what happens when systems are healthy.

In the post-pandemic moment, we're relearning this. Distributed work. Distributed teams. Distributed knowledge. Centralization is brittle. Scatter is resilient. A network of scattered nodes survives the loss of any single node. A centralized hub collapses when the center is threatened.

Design's role is not to impose false order on scatter. It's to reveal the hidden order already present. To show which scattered points matter most. To connect them with invisible lines. To help us see constellations instead of chaos. The scatter isn't the problem. How we perceive it is everything.
