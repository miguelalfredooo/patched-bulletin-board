**The Emotional Power of Scatter**

A carefully spaced grid feels controlled, even sterile. Scattered elements feel more alive. They suggest plurality, accident, emergence. When dots are randomly distributed, the eye searches for meaning, and in that search, engagement happens.

Psychologically, scatter mirrors how we actually experience information—not in orderly columns but in streams, feeds, collections. Instagram's grid tried to impose order but failed because the content is inherently scattered. TikTok doesn't pretend otherwise. Scatter admits the truth: ideas come in fragments.

This is why playlists scatter songs. Why photo albums now show images scattered across timelines. Why typography is moving from rigid systems to responsive, distributed layouts. Scatter feels more honest. More human. More like how meaning actually assembles.
