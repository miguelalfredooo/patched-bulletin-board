**Composition Without Grid: How Scatter Becomes Design**

The Swiss design movement perfected the grid. Every element in its place. Rational. Controlled. But something was lost: the organic, the unexpected, the scattered. Contemporary designers are reclaiming scatter as a compositional tool.

It's not about abandoning structure. It's about structure that tolerates variation. Some elements anchored, others floating. Some precise, some approximate. The result feels both organized and alive. This is how nature designs: distributed systems with local rules, not global control.

Designers like Experimental Jetset and studio practices in Berlin have made scatter a luxury. It takes more skill to make scattered elements feel intentional than to arrange them in a grid. Each placement must justify itself without the safety of alignment. The scatter must *mean* something, even if that meaning is "organized chaos."
