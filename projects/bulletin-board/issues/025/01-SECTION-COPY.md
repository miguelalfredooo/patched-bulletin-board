**The Universe as Scatter: Order Within Dispersal**

Stars don't arrange themselves in neat rows. They scatter. Galaxies spiral. Solar systems tumble through space without symmetry or plan. Yet we see patterns. We see the Big Dipper. We see Orion. The scatter contains order—a human order imposed by attention and storytelling.

Contemporary design is learning what the cosmos always knew: scatter isn't chaos, it's possibility. A grid is boring. Scattered elements feel alive, distributed, more true to how things actually exist. The universe is the ultimate scatter composition.

Designers now study stellar distribution, particle systems, and natural randomness to build interfaces that feel less controlled and more human. The precision we once prized—perfect alignment, perfect spacing—has given way to intentional scatter. Rules that allow for variation. Systems that permit beautiful accident.
