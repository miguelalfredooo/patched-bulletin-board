**Constellations: How We Connect Scattered Points**

The night sky is scattered stars, but we draw lines between them. We see bears and hunters and heroes. The scatter itself is neutral. Meaning comes from connection.

This is how we understand data now. Raw information is scattered—millions of data points. Meaning emerges when we draw lines between them. Data visualization is the art of revealing which scattered points should be connected and which should stand alone. The scatter is the raw material. The lines are the story.

In social media, users are scattered points. Connection—following, friending, liking—creates constellations. The platform doesn't create the points. It reveals the lines between them. This is why recommendation algorithms matter so much. They choose which distant points to connect, which scattered users to bring into constellation with each other.
