**Scatter in Data: Noise and Signal**

Big data is scatter. Millions of data points with no obvious meaning. The skill is finding signal in the scatter. Drawing lines between scattered points that reveal pattern. Most data analysis is the art of making sense of scatter.

Machine learning is scatter processing. You feed it scattered examples. It finds the hidden structures. The patterns that connect. It doesn't eliminate the scatter. It learns to navigate it, to find relationships you couldn't see manually.

This is why data visualization matters so much. It takes scattered numbers and makes them visible—plots them in space so you can see the pattern. The scatter itself doesn't change. Your perception of it does. Good visualization reveals the order hidden within apparent randomness.
