**Scatter in Architecture: Decentered Space**

The classical building has a center. A symmetrical facade, a central atrium, a clear hierarchy. Contemporary architecture scatters functions. The entry is not where you'd expect. The spaces don't announce their purpose clearly. You have to navigate, to discover, to orient yourself.

This can feel disorienting. But it's also more human. It rewards exploration. It creates micro-experiences scattered throughout the space. The user has agency in determining their path. The scatter doesn't impose order. It allows multiple valid experiences.

Firms like Rem Koolhaas and Zaha Hadid design scattered spaces intentionally. Not chaos. But a different kind of order—one that emerges from how you move through it rather than being imposed from above. The scatter is the structure.
