**Scatter in Time: How Attention Disperses**

The average person checks their phone 96 times per day. Our attention scatters across notifications, platforms, messages. We're all experiencing scatter as a lived condition. Not metaphorical—actual, neurological scatter.

This isn't moral failure. It's adaptation. Our ancestors had attention anchored to a single task, a single place. Now we're wired for scatter. We switch contexts rapidly. We maintain multiple conversations simultaneously. We exist in distributed attention.

Design has to account for this. Apps that demand focus fail. Apps that tolerate quick dips and scatter succeed. The rhythm of attention is scattered, and design has to match the rhythm of how humans actually think now. Not complaint. Observation. The new normal is scatter.
