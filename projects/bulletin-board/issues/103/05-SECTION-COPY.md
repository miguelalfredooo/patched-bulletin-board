**Scatter as Community: Distributed Belonging**

Medieval villages had a center—the church, the square. People clustered there. Now we're scattered. Remote work, global communities, friendships across continents. We belong to communities we never physically gather with.

This isn't isolation. It's a different topology of belonging. You're connected to people scattered across the world through shared interests, shared platforms, shared practices. The old gathering place is gone. The scattered network is the new gathering place.

Design enables this. Discord servers scatter members across channels. Slack channels scatter conversations. Platforms scatter communities. The scattered structure isn't a limitation—it's precisely what enables global community. You can belong to many scattered communities simultaneously. Connection without geographic proximity. Friendship without shared location.
