**Scatter in Resilience: Distributed Systems Survive**

Centralized systems are fragile. One failure at the center collapses everything. But scattered, distributed systems are resilient. Node failures don't cascade. The network survives because no single node is essential.

This is why the internet was designed scattered. No central authority means no single point of failure. Blockchain technology embraces scatter—every node holds a copy. If one fails, thousands of others persist. Scatter is strategy.

This has design implications. Avoid single points of failure. Distribute authentication. Scatter data across servers. Design systems where parts can fail independently. Scatter isn't inefficiency. It's insurance. It's betting that resilience matters more than optimization, and in our unstable moment, scatter is the right bet.
