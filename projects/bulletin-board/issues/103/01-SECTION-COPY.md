**Scatter as Physics: The Natural State of Things**

Objects at rest stay at rest until a force acts on them. Then they scatter. A dandelion seed head breaks apart. A flock explodes at the sound of a branch. Scatter isn't disorder—it's the physical response to change. It's natural. It's inevitable.

In nature, scatter is success. Seeds that scatter far have better survival odds than seeds that cluster. Animals that scatter across territory have better access to resources. Systems that distribute themselves are more robust than centralized ones.

Contemporary designers watch nature and see: scatter is not a failure state. It's an optimization. The universe prefers distribution. We keep trying to impose order, and nature keeps showing us: order is local. Scatter is the truth. Everything eventually disperses.
