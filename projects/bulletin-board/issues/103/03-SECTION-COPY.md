**Visual Scatter in Contemporary Art: Breaking the Rectangle**

Paintings hung on gallery walls in grids. Sculptures arranged on pedestals in linear rows. We imposed order on art. Now, artists scatter their work deliberately: installations across rooms, sculptures at different heights, paintings at angles.

This scatter is liberation. The artist releases the work into space rather than constraining it to the wall. The viewer has to move, to walk, to find multiple perspectives. The scatter makes the space collaborative—your position in the room determines your experience of the work. There is no single correct view.

This mirrors how we experience art digitally. Instagram feeds scatter images. TikTok scatters videos. Playlists scatter songs. The rectangle of the frame is broken. Art exists in scattered collections now, and that scatter is actually more honest about how we encounter art: piecemeal, scattered across time and feeds.
