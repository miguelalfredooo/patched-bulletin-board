**Scatter in Meaning: Interpretation as Navigation**

A single artwork viewed once has one meaning. The same artwork scattered across time, viewed from different angles, in different moods, with different knowledge—has infinite meanings. Scatter creates possibility.

This is why remixes matter. Why sampling matters. A song scattered into different contexts becomes different songs. Meaning isn't fixed in the object. It emerges from the scatter of object, context, observer, and moment. Everything is reinterpretable.

Contemporary meaning-making is scattered. We don't inherit fixed meanings. We construct them from scattered sources. A single image, scattered across platforms, accumulates different meanings in each context. Meaning is now distributed, negotiated, scattered. Design has to account for this ambiguity.
