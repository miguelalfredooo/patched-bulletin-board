**Scatter as Beginning: Where Everything Starts**

In the beginning, there was scatter. Particles scattered across the void. They gathered into atoms, atoms into molecules, molecules into cells, cells into life. Scatter is the fundamental state. Order is what emerges temporarily.

Every creation myth knows this. Chaos precedes form. The chaos isn't evil. It's potential. The scatter is where everything is possible before it becomes something specific. To scatter is to return to potential. To scatter is to begin again.

Design thinks too much about finishing—polishing, refining, complete. But scatter reminds us: beginnings are messy. Potential is scattered. The most creative moment is when everything is scattered and the pattern hasn't emerged yet. Hold that moment. Stay scattered long enough to find unexpected patterns. Then assemble what you found.
