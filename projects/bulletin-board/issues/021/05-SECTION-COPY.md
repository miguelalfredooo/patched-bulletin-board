**The New Gathering Place: From Home to Office to City**

The pandemic scrambled our geography. Office workers discovered they could work from anywhere. Suddenly, the coffee shop wasn't a destination—it was an option. Gathering became voluntary, not mandatory.

Now we're redesigning everywhere at once. The office adds soft seating and quiet nooks. The coffee shop adds workspace tables. Parks install long benches that invite groups. Cities reclaim car lanes for patios and plazas. Each space asks: why would people *want* to be here together?

The answer is always objects. A table that invites lingering. A bench that says "stay awhile." A counter that encourages conversation. A fireplace that gathers. These aren't decoration—they're infrastructure for human connection.

Companies like Steelcase and manufacturers worldwide are rethinking the social object for a new era. Higher seats (sight lines). Softer materials (tactility). Mobile bases (flexibility). The lesson: people will gather if we make gathering feel good.
