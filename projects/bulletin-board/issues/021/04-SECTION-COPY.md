**Testing Togetherness: How Designers Prototype Connection**

For decades, designers tested objects in isolation—does it work? Can you use it? Today's best practices ask a harder question: does it work *together*? How do people move around it? When do they linger? When do they leave?

New tools make this testable. Proximity sensors measure comfortable distance. Video analysis reveals natural flow patterns. Thermal imaging shows where warmth happens. AI observation catches moments of spontaneous joy—the laugh, the lean-in, the shared look.

At IDEO and other forward-thinking studios, prototyping now means watching groups of people inhabit a space or gather around an object. The designer observes: where do conversations start? Where do they stall? What invites people closer? What pushes them apart?

This is design as hypothesis testing. Every line, every curve, every material choice is a prediction about human behavior. We test by watching people prove us right or wrong.
