**Visual Thinking: How We Perceive Social Objects**

Objects speak before we touch them. A round table signals openness; everyone faces everyone. A high counter creates distance; a barrier between server and guest. Color, material, scale—all communicate whether an object is made for solitude or gathering.

Designers understand that visual cues trigger social behavior. A coffee cup suggests pause and conversation. A bench with armrests says "alone together." The shape of a room, the height of a chair, the warmth of wood—these aren't decorative choices. They're social invitations embedded in form.

The best social objects are visually honest about their purpose. We don't need to think about why we want to sit at a round table with friends. The design has already told us.
