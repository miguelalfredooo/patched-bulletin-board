**Surfaces That Welcome Touch: Material as Social Language**

Wood ages differently than metal. Leather develops a patina. Stone offers temperature. These aren't details—they're invitations. A rough wooden table says "this can handle use." Soft linen says "this is made for touch." Cold steel says "this is about function, not comfort."

Haptics—the science of touch—reveals a truth designers have always known: we judge objects by how they feel. A social object must want to be touched, worn, used. Material tells the story. A well-chosen surface accumulates stories. Marks, stains, dents—they become proof that the object has done its job: brought people together enough to leave traces.

The worst social objects are sterile, untouchable, designed to repel use. The best are materials that improve with time, that show their gathering scars as badges of honor.
