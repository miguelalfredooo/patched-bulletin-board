**The Conversation You Can't Refuse: How Good Design Forces Connection**

Some social objects are so well-designed that refusing to use them together becomes awkward. A round table at dinner. A fireplace in winter. A long bench. The design makes solitude difficult and togetherness inevitable.

This isn't manipulation—it's clarity. A well-designed social object removes friction from human connection. No one has to negotiate why they're sitting together. The object has already made the case. A low table invites feet to stretch, bodies to lean in. A shared armrest creates unavoidable proximity.

The best designers understand: the object does the social work before the conversation begins. By then, the design has already won.
