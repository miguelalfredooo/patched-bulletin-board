**Lighting the Gathering: How Illumination Creates Warmth**

Anthropologists have always known: humans gather toward fire. Not just for heat, but for the feeling. Light creates a perimeter. A fireplace, a candle, a lamp—these define the space where togetherness happens.

Modern social objects often fail here. The table is functional but cold. The counter is efficient but unwelcoming. Contemporary designers are relearning what every historical gathering space knew: light creates intimacy.

A warm ceiling fixture, fireplace glow, or candlelit table transforms a room's social geometry. Suddenly, proximity feels natural instead of imposed. The light doesn't just illuminate faces—it signals: this is a place where it's safe to linger, to look at each other, to be seen.
