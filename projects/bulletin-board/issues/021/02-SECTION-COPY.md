**Rituals Without Words: The Global Coffee Cup**

Across the world, the same ritual repeats: boil water, pour into a vessel, sit together. The coffee ceremony in Ethiopia. The tea house in Japan. The espresso bar in Rome. The specific beverage changes, but the meaning stays constant—this vessel is permission to pause.

Anthropologist Mary Douglas called such moments "social glue." The object itself is almost irrelevant. What matters is what the object *means*: "I am making time for you. Let's be here together." A cup is translation. No words needed.

When we design social objects, we're not designing containers. We're designing permission structures. The cup says: slow down. The table says: face each other. The bench says: you belong here.
