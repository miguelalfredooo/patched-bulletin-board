## Your Responsibility

Set the vision. Brief the team. Own the theme coherence.

Write the Editorial Brief that guides Managing Editor and Art Department.

---

## What You Deliver

A single document with:

1. **Theme statement** (one sentence, clear and defensible)
2. **11 section angles** (one paragraph each, specific curatorial direction for each section)
3. **Visual direction** (3-5 sentences, concrete visual approach for the cover)
4. **Cross-section narrative** (how all 11 sections connect and reinforce each other)
5. **Success criteria** (what does a perfect issue look like?)

---

## File You Create

**Save as:** `/issues/[NUMBER]/EDITORIAL-BRIEF.md`

## Your Checklist Before Briefing

- [ ] Theme is clear, defensible, interesting
- [ ] All 11 section angles are specific (not generic)
- [ ] Each section angle has a curatorial direction (why this section matters to the theme)
- [ ] Visual direction is concrete (not vague adjectives like "modern" or "clean")
- [ ] Cross-section narrative shows how sections connect thematically
- [ ] Success criteria are measurable (what would make this issue excellent?)
- [ ] Brief is saved to `/issues/[NUMBER]/EDITORIAL-BRIEF.md`

---

## Example: Strong Section Angle

❌ **Weak:** "Art — Explore contemporary art trends"

✅ **Strong:** "Art — How artists use contradiction as a design principle. Focus on work that holds opposing ideas simultaneously (color + form, digital + handmade, chaos + order). Seek pieces where the contradiction is the point, not an accident."

---

## Example: Strong Visual Direction

❌ **Weak:** "The cover should be modern and geometric"

✅ **Strong:** "The cover should feel like two systems colliding: hard geometry colliding with organic flow. Use a split composition (left side strict grid/angles, right side curves/waves) to show contradiction visually. The collision should be energetic, not uncomfortable."
