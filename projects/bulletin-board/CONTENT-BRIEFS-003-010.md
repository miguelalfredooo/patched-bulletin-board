## ISSUE 003 — Handmade
**Theme:** Lo-fi rebellion, imperfection as stance
**Closing Sentence:** "The machine can't feel the thumb leave a ridge in the clay."

### Editorial Positioning
This issue asserts imperfection as a deliberate editorial stance—not failure, but refusal. Split nibs, coil-built clay, enlarger work, hand annotation breaking the grid: all are acts of resistance to machine-generated slickness. Active cultural signal: New Yorker's "Lo-Fi Rebellion Against A.I." (live this week), Frieze New York concurrent.

### Section Assignments & Prose Briefs

| Section | Format | Theme/Angle | Prose Description (ACT 2 basis) |
|---------|--------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| **Art** | A | The handmade object as refusal | Find work that asserts human imperfection as central claim, not apologetic footnote. One artist, one clear gesture of resistance. |
| **Painting** | D | Paint as trace of gesture | Brush marks that cannot be replicated by algorithm. Medium specificity. Show the hand's work, not the finished image alone. |
| **Illustration** | A | Drawing without correction tools | Pen and paper only. The line that stays wrong because fixing it is the point. Analog-first practice. |
| **Sculpture** | B | Clay and the hand | Coil-built, slab-constructed, hand-pressed. Material memory of touch. The thumb ridge as signature. |
| **Culture** | E | Lo-fi as movement, not aesthetic | The deliberate choice to be imperfect. Folk pottery ethics. Zines. Hand-bound. Small-run publications rejecting polish. |
| **Photography** | A | The grain as refusal | Film stock chosen for visible grain. The imperfection desired, not fixed in post. Shooting for the "mistakes." |
| **Art History** | B | Craft tradition as counter-narrative | Medieval illumination, folk pottery lineages, Shaker precision through hand work. The imperfect as part of a continuum. |
| **Opinions** | C | The slowness case | Tool culture essay: why choosing slow methods matters. Rejection of optimization. Friction as feature. |
| **Design & AI Tools** | D | Hand-coded as aesthetic choice | Design systems built by hand. Type foundries returning to manual processes. Tools that slow you down intentionally. |
| **Product & Process** | A | Making visible | Maker interviews, studio visits, the process documented as final product. Behind-the-scenes as the work itself. |
| **Visual & Brand** | E | Typography of imperfection | Typefaces designed to show variation, error, handwriting models. Fonts that "feel" human. |

### Thematic Notes
Distinct from **Issue 004** (Traces — evidence of presence) and **Issue 009** (Material — substance itself). Handmade is about **deliberate imperfection as a stance**: the split nib chosen, the grain visible, the seam proud. It is not accidental. It is political.

## ISSUE 008 — Threshold
**Theme:** Liminal edge, figure at the cliff, decision-before-commitment
**Closing Sentence:** "Every threshold is a place where you are still both things at once."

### Editorial Positioning
The liminal edge—the vestibule, the decision-before-commitment, the figure at the cliff, light entering a frame, the note arriving from a distance, toggle between states. Springford's rediscovery and Friedrich's Rückenfigur anchor the theme. Nengudi's nylon holds without giving. Kawauchi's light crosses over. The threshold is not a problem to solve—it is where the most interesting design happens. Deliberate pace without stillness. ECM sonic register.

## ISSUE 009 — Material

### Editorial Positioning
The irreducible fact of substance—bodily materials as medium, clay and soil as architecture, analogue resistance in graphic design, film grain as subject, paper prototyping over digital, NIGO's fabric-first identity. Material not as aesthetic but as constraint, argument, and presence. Theme distinct from Handmade (imperfection as stance) and Traces (evidence of presence). ## ISSUE 010 — Worn
**Theme:** Time-in-contact, what happens when things are genuinely used
**Closing Sentence:** "The things worth keeping are the things that show you used them."

### Editorial Positioning
The accumulation of use as its own form of meaning. Sherald's raw linen holds the grisaille. Brown's figures dissolve into the ground. Nengudi's mesh strains without tearing. Fukase's ravens leave sky behind. Yanagi's unsigned pots. Tools that wear in rather than reset. Worn is not tired. Worn is evidence. Distinct from Handmade (imperfection as stance), Traces (evidence of presence), and Material (substance itself). Worn is about **time-in-contact**: what happens to things—and tools, and images—when they've been genuinely used.

## Guidance for Managing Editor

### Prose Development
Each section description above is the **foundation** for ACT 2 prose. The Managing Editor should:
1. Write 1-2 sentences (usually 2) per section
2. Keep sentences specific to the actual curated work (not generic to the theme)
3. Use the "Prose Description" angle to guide research and writing depth
4. Ensure each sentence could only describe that specific piece on that specific day

### Source Tracking
Refer to **BRIEF.md** and **SOURCES.md** for approved publications. No source repeats within a single issue (except music). Track usage across all 11 sections.

### Format Assignments
The Format letters (A-E) correspond to ASCII rendering formats defined in ASCII-COMPOSITION-GUIDE.md:
- **A** — Sparse Object, Light
- **B** — Geometric Frame, Heavy  
- **C** — Typographic, Medium
- **D** — Two Column, Medium
- **E** — Full Spread, Heavy

No consecutive sections repeat the same format. Visual weight should vary naturally.

### Theme Integration
The theme should **never be stated explicitly** within the prose. It should be **felt** through:
- Section choice and curation specificity
- Prose word choice and rhythm
- Visual form and composition
- Convergence of examples (without explaining the convergence)

### Editorial Mix Application
Reference the EDITORIAL MIX percentages in archive-log.md for each issue. These modulate:
- How much Music influences prose rhythm and tempo
- How much Visual drives section ordering
- How much Research intensity affects source synthesis depth
- How much Process/maker perspective dominates
- Whether Theme is felt or explicit
- How much AI/tool culture features
