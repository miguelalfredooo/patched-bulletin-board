NOTES ON APERTURE

An aperture controls what passes through. It opens to reveal,
closes to contain. The cycle is eternal: sealed → opening →
open → closing → sealed → repeat.

In design, apertures are moments of access. A well-composed
aperture lets the viewer see what matters, for as long as it
matters, then closes gently.

The issue follows the gallery/catalog pattern of variable scale
and weight counterbalancing:

• Section 1: Heavy opening (dense frame) — complete seal
• Section 2: Light crack (sparse) — first opening
• Section 3: Medium threshold (two-column) — between states
• Section 4: Heavy iris (geometric) — aperture mechanism visible
• Section 5: Minimal breath (one point) — pause
• Section 6: Typography (medium) — light entering
• Section 7: Heavy radiating (expanding) — opening widening
• Section 8: Sparse peak (three points) — moment of maximum
• Section 9: Very heavy climax (full spread) — completely open
• Section 10: Medium closing (two-column) — returning
• Section 11: Minimal seal (sparse) — closure complete

Scale varies: 10 lines → 3 lines → 6 lines → 8 lines → 1 line →
3 lines → 8 lines → 3 lines → 12 lines → 7 lines → 3 lines.

No consecutive sections repeat. The composition breathes.
The eye knows where to pause. The theme guides the form.
