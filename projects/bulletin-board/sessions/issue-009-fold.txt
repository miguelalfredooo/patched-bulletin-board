NOTES ON FOLD

A fold is not a break. It is compression and revelation in sequence.
When you fold paper, nothing is destroyed—it is hidden temporarily,
waiting to be revealed. The pattern remains intact beneath the surface.

In design, in code, in thought: compression reveals structure.
What matters is what cannot be removed, what survives the fold.

The issue follows the gallery/catalog pattern of variable scale and
weight counterbalancing:

• Section 1: Heavy opening (dense frame) — establishes theme
• Section 2: Light reset (sparse object) — breathing room
• Section 3: Medium building — continues the narrative
• Section 4: Dense nested boxes — climax approach
• Section 5: Minimal breath — pause point
• Section 6: Typography — shift in approach
• Section 7: Heavy radiating — visual weight building
• Section 8: Sparse separation — breathing room
• Section 9: Heaviest climax (full spread) — visual peak
• Section 10: Medium resolution — coming down
• Section 11: Minimal close — gentle landing

No two consecutive sections share the same visual weight or scale.
The eye pauses naturally. The composition breathes.
