ACT 2 — FULL EDITION

**Art**
At Frieze New York this week, Ane Graff's white-gloved handlers transfer work so delicately you wonder if the precaution is for the piece or the people touching it. https://www.frieze.com/fairs/frieze-new-york

**Painting**
Lucas Arruda's blurred, haze-gray seascapes have no horizon you can fix on — the mark of the brush is the only evidence anyone was there. https://www.artsy.net/artist/lucas-arruda

**Illustration**
Marzena Szumlas hand-draws her lettering so slowly the ink bleeds slightly at each stop, and that imprecision is precisely the point. https://www.itsnicethat.com

**Sculpture**
Isa Genzken's assemblages at the Venice Architecture Biennale hold broken umbrellas and foil sheeting inside steel frames like evidence from a scene — the material is the statement, the scale is the argument. https://www.frieze.com/article/venice-architecture-biennale-2025

**Culture**
The New Yorker tracks designers leaving deliberate typos, handwritten credits, and prison-tattoo covers as proof of human authorship in a year when every smooth surface reads as machine-made. https://www.newyorker.com

**Photography**
Tarrah Krajnak's series of self-portraits made on expired film uses the chemistry's own failures — fog, bloom, dissolution — as the register of time passing. https://www.lensculture.com

**Art History**
In 1960, Yves Klein pressed gold-painted models into canvas while an orchestra played twenty minutes of silence; the press called it a stunt, and the marks are still in museums. https://www.jstor.org/stable/fine-arts

**Opinions**
Kyle Chayka argues in The New Yorker that "tasteslop" — AI-generated content indistinguishable from human curation — has made the conspicuous typo and the rough edge into signals of authenticity rather than signs of error. https://www.newyorker.com/culture/infinite-scroll

**Design & AI Tools**
Anthropic's new citation feature in Claude traces which part of a source document each sentence came from — making the model's evidence legible rather than implied. https://www.anthropic.com/news

**Product & Process**
Lenny Rachitsky argues that the most durable design decisions are the ones with a visible author — someone who can say "I made this choice because" — and that anonymizing process is how bad defaults survive. https://www.lennysnewsletter.com

**Visual & Brand**
Apartamento's "Reference Library" installation at Milan's Fuorisalone asked visitors to wear white gloves to handle books that shaped how the magazine sees — a brand gesture that turned reading into ceremony, and the glove into a logo. https://www.apartamento-magazine.com
