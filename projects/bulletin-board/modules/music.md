# Module: Music & Sound Culture
## Design by Bulletin

**Purpose:** Surface emerging sound as a cultural signal — not a playlist,
not a recommendation engine. One hero track and three picks that together
map where aesthetics are heading 3–6 months from now.

**Sources:** Bandcamp, Discogs, Spotify, YouTube Music
**Exception:** Music sources may repeat within a single issue.
Different artists or playlists from the same platform are always OK.

**Philosophy:** Obscure and up-and-coming only. No charting artists.
No algorithmically popular picks. The signal lives at the edges.

**Structure — four tracks per issue:**

1. **Hero track** — one artist, one sentence of context, one link.
   The track that sets the issue's sonic register. Chosen by the
   Editorial Director for its relationship to the day's theme.

2. **Pick 01** — shorter. One clause, one link.
3. **Pick 02** — shorter. One clause, one link.
4. **Pick 03** — shorter. One clause, one link.

The three picks extend the hero's argument — they don't repeat it.
Different tempos, different textures, same frequency.

**Tone:** Same Apartamento register as the rest of the digest.
Notice the specific thing. Name the artist, the release, the moment.
Never: "this is a great listen." Never: "check this out."

**Writing rules:**
- Hero track gets one full sentence — enough room for one specific observation
- Picks get one clause — tight, no setup
- No genre labels unless the label is itself the observation
- Sound described through its effect, not its category

**ASCII format:** E — Full Spread or D — Two Column.
The hero track gets an ASCII piece that captures its sonic register —
not a literal instrument, but the texture, frequency, or mood of the sound.
Sparse braille halftone for ambient/minimal. Dense fills for noisy/industrial.
Two-column for artist + initial or sound + silence. Full spread for panoramic sound.
Generate via the same ASCII construction rules as all other sections.

**Placement:** Closes the issue, after Visual & Brand.
Music is the last thing — it carries the theme out of the digest
and into the reader's day.

**Agent roles:**
- Editorial Director: Sets daily vibe, selects hero track
- Maeve: Detects cultural signals (emerging sound → emerging aesthetic)
- Victor: Translates sound to visual register for ASCII composition
- Managing Editor: Uses sonic landscape to shape prose pacing across all sections

**Version:** 1.0.0
