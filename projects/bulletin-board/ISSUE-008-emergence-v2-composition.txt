ACT 2 — NARRATIVE & REFLECTION

───────────────────────────────────────────

On Rhythm and Breathing

In typography, leading (line height) creates readability. In ASCII composition,
negative space and blank lines are your leading. They control how fast the reader
moves down the page.

Version 2 demonstrates pacing:
• Sections 1-2: DENSE then SPARSE (heavy relief needed)
• Sections 3-5: BUILDING momentum through repetition and acceleration
• Section 6: FULL PAUSE — the reader must stop and breathe
• Sections 7-11: RISING again — but with more contemplation

The vertical rhythm isn't arbitrary. Each element's weight is chosen to guide
the eye and control the reading experience. A sparse section after density isn't
an accident—it's compositional intent.

───────────────────────────────────────────

 Composition Principles Applied:
 • Vertical Weight Balance: Heavy ↔ Light creates rhythm
 • Breathing Room: Blank lines control pace, not accidents
 • Eye Guidance: Size variation creates natural reading flow
 • Negative Space: Emptiness is design, not waste
 • Pacing: Reader must pause after intensity, accelerate through calm
 • Format Sequence: No consecutive repeats create variety
 • Visual Climax: Section 5 (Format E) is densest and most complex
 • Resolution: Return to simplicity in final section
