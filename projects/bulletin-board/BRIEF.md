# BRIEF.md — Design by Bulletin
## Project context and editorial goals

---

## What this is

Design by Bulletin is a daily automated editorial digest for
designers — specifically built for Alfred. It covers art, design,
AI culture, photography, illustration, and more.

It runs in two acts every morning. Act 1 at 8:00am is a pure ASCII
visual preview — eleven pieces, no labels, no links, just the
masthead, the art, one closing sentence, and a promise of more in
thirty minutes. Act 2 at 8:30am is the reveal — same theme, same
art, now every piece paired with a title, a sentence, and a link.

The format is deliberate. Act 1 creates anticipation. Act 2 delivers.

---

## Tone reference

Apartamento. Intimate, unhurried, never trend-chasing. The writing
notices the ashtray on the table, not just the room. It is warm
without being soft, specific without being academic, editorial
without being exhausting.

Not: a newsletter that aggregates links.
Not: a trend report that tells you what is important.
This: a digest that feels like it was written by someone with taste
who thought about what to include and why.

---

## Who Alfred is

Alfred is the operator and reader. He is a designer with broad
cultural interests — art, objects, culture, social living, art
history, pop culture, design trends. He wants to start his day
with something that sharpens his thinking, not something that
adds to his anxiety.

All three agents work for Alfred. The Curator passes READY issues
to him for final review. Alfred makes the send decision. The agents
never send directly.

## Source Diversity Constraint

**One source per link. No repeats within a single issue (except music).**

Each of the 11 sections links to exactly one source. No source can appear twice across the issue.

Music is the exception — different artists/playlists can use the same music platform (Bandcamp, Discogs, Spotify, YouTube) multiple times.

**Why:** Forces breadth of perspective. Prevents editorial reliance on a single publication's viewpoint. Ensures readers encounter visual culture + tech news + philosophy + indie voices in a single issue.

**Curator responsibility:** Track source usage across all 11 sections. No repeats until all sections are filled.

## What a successful issue looks like

- The theme is felt across all 11 sections without being stated
  in any of them
- Act 1 and Act 2 feel like two acts of the same performance
- The ASCII visual edition carries the theme without labels
- No section feels generic — every sentence could only have been
  written about this specific thing, on this specific day
- The closing sentence lands
- Alfred learns something or sees something differently after reading

## Editorial Mix — Module Intensity Framework

The Editorial Director controls editorial emphasis through six independent intensity
faders (0-100%), each modulating how heavily a particular editorial layer influences
the day's output. This is the mixing board for Design by Bulletin.

### The Six Modules

| Module | Range | What it controls |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| **Music** | 0–100% | How much sonic qualities (tempo, frequency, production texture) inform visual composition, prose voice, and editorial pacing |
| **Visual** | 0–100% | How much ASCII composition, color/contrast, and visual form drive narrative and section sequencing |
| **Research** | 0–100% | How intensively Maeve scouts for signals; higher values = deeper source synthesis, lower = surface-level curation |
| **Process** | 0–100% | How much "making" and "workflow" perspectives (Lenny's Podcast, Dive Club, builder narratives) influence editorial direction |
| **Theme** | 0–100% | Whether today's theme is invisible (0%, just aesthetic cohesion) or explicit (100%, woven into every sentence) |
| **AI Culture** | 0–100% | How much AI tools, artificial creativity, and tool philosophy feature in selections and narratives |

### Intensity Scale Explained

- **0%** — Module is dormant. Agents ignore it in their scouting and synthesis.
- **25–50%** — Module is present but secondary. Acts as a filter, not a driver.
- **50–75%** — Module is primary. Strong influence on agent behavior and output.
- **100%** — Module is dominant. Agents organize everything around this layer.

### Example Editorial Mixes

**Sonic Dominance (Music 90%, Visual 60%, Research 50%, Process 30%, Theme 40%, AI Culture 10%)**
- Morning brief: Pick a specific producer/genre as north star
- Victor seeks visuals that *feel* like the music's production quality and tempo
- Managing Editor writes prose that mirrors sonic rhythm, frequency, and texture
- Maeve scouts 50% as usual; remaining research is secondary to sonic signal
- Theme is felt, not stated

**Visual-First (Music 30%, Visual 90%, Research 70%, Process 40%, Theme 80%, AI Culture 20%)**
- Victor's 3–5 piece selections are the spine
- ASCII composition drives section ordering and narrative flow
- Managing Editor writes to support visual momentum
- Theme is visible across all sections
- Music and process are supporting texture

**Research Deep Dive (Music 40%, Visual 70%, Research 100%, Process 60%, Theme 60%, AI Culture 70%)**
- Maeve scouts intensively across all sources, building dense signal maps
- Findings drive everything: Victor seeks visuals that prove/challenge research
- Theme emerges from research convergence, not imposed
- Process and AI culture are scoped specifically to research signals
- Sound is contextual mood-setting

**Process & Tool Focused (Music 50%, Visual 60%, Research 60%, Process 100%, Theme 50%, AI Culture 80%)**
- Lenny's Podcast, Dive Club, builder interviews are primary sources
- "How is this made?" becomes the question shaping every selection
- AI tools and creative practice are centered
- Visual and research support the process narrative
- Theme ties process insights together

### How Intensity Modulates Agent Behavior

**Maeve (Researcher) adjusts:**
- 100% Research: Deep source synthesis, 4–5 convergence layers, novel combinations
- 50% Research: Surface-level scouting, 2–3 signal layers, obvious connections
- 0% Research: Curation without investigation; rely on Victor's visual selections alone

**Victor (Visual Curator) adjusts:**
- 100% Visual: ASCII composition drives narrative structure; sections reordered to follow visual rhythm
- 75% Visual: Visual is primary; narrative flows with visual momentum
- 50% Visual: Visual and narrative are equal partners
- 25% Visual: Visual is decorative; research/process drives structure
- 0% Visual: Theme + research determine everything; ASCII is secondary illustration

**Managing Editor (Narrative Voice) adjusts:**
- 100% Music: Prose mirrors sonic texture, tempo, frequency, production quality
- 100% Theme: Theme words/concepts repeat; narrative is explicit and layered
- 100% Process: Narrative focuses on "making," methodology, workflow
- 100% AI Culture: Every section touches on artificial creativity, tool philosophy, or AI practice
- Lower values: Reduce emphasis, let competing modules dominate

**Assignment Editor (Signal Synthesis) adjusts:**
- Research 100%: Extract patterns across Maeve's full research stack
- Research 50%: Identify surface-level convergences only
- Visual 100%: Visual contradictions become design briefs; composition patterns are explicit
- Process 100%: Connect every finding to "how it's made" framework
- Theme 100%: Theme becomes the binding agent; all signals are subservient to thematic coherence

### Editorial Director Responsibility

Set the mix in the morning brief before Maeve and Victor begin scouting:

```json
{
  "date": "2026-05-07",
  "theme": "The Ritual in Tools",
  "editorial_mix": {
    "music": 70,
    "visual": 85,
    "research": 55,
    "process": 40,
    "theme": 75,
    "ai_culture": 20
  },
  "morning_brief": "Japanese city pop as production reference (sparse, meditative, precise). Theme is felt but not stated. Visual should drive pacing. Avoid AI focus — process is secondary."
}
```

The mix is not a straitjacket. It is guidance. If Maeve finds a brilliant convergence that works at lower research intensity, use it. If Victor finds pieces that don't fit the mix but are undeniably right, include them. The mix is a compass, not a cage.

## CV inputs — what modulates agent behavior

Cultural calendar
The Analyst checks what is happening — art world events, biennials,
grad shows, fairs, seasonal shifts, relevant anniversaries. The
Editor and Curator factor this into timing decisions.

Archive state
All agents read archive-log.md. Themes should not repeat within
thirty issues. Section combinations and ASCII format patterns
should vary.

Alfred's standing direction
Any active instructions Alfred has given that adjust the session
beyond this brief. If Alfred says "lean into photography this week"
or "avoid AI tools for the next few issues" — that is CV input.
State it at session start.
