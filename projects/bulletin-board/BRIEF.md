# BRIEF.md — Design by Bulletin
## Project context and editorial goals

---

## What this is

Design by Bulletin is a daily automated editorial digest for
designers — specifically built for Alfred. It covers art, design,
AI culture, photography, illustration, and more.

It runs in two acts every morning. Act 1 at 8:00am is a pure ASCII
visual preview — eleven pieces, no labels, no links, just the
masthead, the art, one closing sentence, and a promise of more in
thirty minutes. Act 2 at 8:30am is the reveal — same theme, same
art, now every piece paired with a title, a sentence, and a link.

The format is deliberate. Act 1 creates anticipation. Act 2 delivers.

---

## Tone reference

Apartamento. Intimate, unhurried, never trend-chasing. The writing
notices the ashtray on the table, not just the room. It is warm
without being soft, specific without being academic, editorial
without being exhausting.

Not: a newsletter that aggregates links.
Not: a trend report that tells you what is important.
This: a digest that feels like it was written by someone with taste
who thought about what to include and why.

---

## Who Alfred is

Alfred is the operator and reader. He is a designer with broad
cultural interests — art, objects, culture, social living, art
history, pop culture, design trends. He wants to start his day
with something that sharpens his thinking, not something that
adds to his anxiety.

All three agents work for Alfred. The Curator passes READY issues
to him for final review. Alfred makes the send decision. The agents
never send directly.

## What a successful issue looks like

- The theme is felt across all 11 sections without being stated
  in any of them
- Act 1 and Act 2 feel like two acts of the same performance
- The ASCII visual edition carries the theme without labels
- No section feels generic — every sentence could only have been
  written about this specific thing, on this specific day
- The closing sentence lands
- Alfred learns something or sees something differently after reading

---

## CV inputs — what modulates agent behavior

Cultural calendar
The Analyst checks what is happening — art world events, biennials,
grad shows, fairs, seasonal shifts, relevant anniversaries. The
Editor and Curator factor this into timing decisions.

Archive state
All agents read archive-log.md. Themes should not repeat within
thirty issues. Section combinations and ASCII format patterns
should vary.

Alfred's standing direction
Any active instructions Alfred has given that adjust the session
beyond this brief. If Alfred says "lean into photography this week"
or "avoid AI tools for the next few issues" — that is CV input.
State it at session start.
