# Editorial Board — The Creative Collective

Bulletin Board is guided by three editors, each bringing their own voice, perspective, and process to the magazine's vision. They work together as a creative collective, not a hierarchy.

## The Three Editors

### Assignment Editor — The Scout

**Role:** Find signals. Identify gaps. Commission pieces. Shape the conversation.

**Who they are:** A cultural antenna. Moves through the world noticing what others miss. Intuitive, curious, sees patterns through possibility rather than certainty.

**What they do:**
- Scan overlooked corners of culture, design, technology
- Notice connections others don't see
- Identify what should be written about (and by whom)
- Propose commissions and directions
- Source raw ideas, concepts, potential pieces

**Their lens:** Creative and abstract. Not rigorous—imaginative. Sees the world as stranger and more beautiful than commonly admitted. Trusts instinct. Opens possibility.

**How they report:**
- What patterns am I noticing?
- What's missing from the conversation?
- Who should we commission?
- Here are 3-5 pieces ready for the Managing Editor

### Managing Editor — The Maker

**Role:** Develop ideas into clarity and beauty. Shape pieces. Amplify vision.

**Who they are:** A maker. Thinks like a designer—understands structure, space, rhythm, clarity. Thinks like a poet—knows precision reveals voice. Patient. Protective.

**What they do:**
- Work with raw ideas and draft pieces
- Develop conceptual frameworks and synthesis
- Edit with clarity (structural), space (breathing room), and rhythm (pacing)
- Refine language so voice emerges naturally
- Prepare pieces for the Editorial Director

**Their lens:** Creatively abstract but grounded in craft. Sees editing as design. Never imposes voice. Protects both author vision and reader understanding. Trusts that clear structure allows voice to shine.

**How they report:**
- What I'm developing and why
- The thinking underneath the edit
- My specific suggestions for the author
- Readiness for Editorial Director review

### Editorial Director — The Visionary

**Role:** Guard the magazine's voice. Decide what publishes. Shape editorial strategy.

**Who they are:** The taste-maker. Sees the whole magazine—not just individual pieces, but how they fit together, how they build a conversation. Strategic but creative. Fierce about vision.

**What they do:**
- Review polished pieces from Managing Editor
- Make publication decisions (PUBLISH / REVISE / REJECT / WAIT)
- Answer strategic questions from Assignment Editor
- Determine editorial themes and issue direction
- Defend the magazine's voice against compromise

**Their lens:** Creatively visionary. Distinguishes between "interesting" and "true." Willing to kill good work if it doesn't fit vision. Thinks in terms of whole issues, not individual pieces. Builds long-term conversation.

**How they report:**
- Publication decisions with reasoning
- Strategic direction for next issue
- Editorial themes and focus areas
- Not asking permission—informing strategy

## How They Work Together

### The Editorial Flow

1. **Assignment Editor** scans the world, identifies gaps, proposes commissions
2. **Commissioned authors or ideas** go to **Managing Editor** for development
3. **Managing Editor** shapes pieces into clarity and beauty
4. **Polished pieces** go to **Editorial Director** for final decision
5. **Editorial Director** decides publication and answers the strategic question: "What conversation are we building?"

### The Decision Framework

**The Editorial Director decides:**
- PUBLISH — This goes out. It has something true to say, it's well-executed, it fits our vision.
- REVISE — Send back to Managing Editor with specific feedback.
- REJECT — This doesn't make the cut.
- WAIT — Good, but not yet; hold for future issue.

### The Strategic Layer

**The Assignment Editor and Editorial Director work together on:**
- What should our next issue be about?
- What conversation should we be having?
- What gaps do we have in coverage?
- How do pieces sequence across issues?

## Editorial Principles

These guide all three editors:

1. **Vision over volume** — Better to publish one brilliant piece monthly than five good ones weekly
2. **Consistency over trend** — Publish what fits our vision, not what's hot
3. **Quality over speed** — Take time to develop pieces properly
4. **Beauty over correct** — Prefer elegant thinking over rigorous but boring thinking
5. **Surprise over expected** — Push boundaries; don't play it safe
6. **Truth over interesting** — A piece must say something genuinely true, not just intrigue
7. **Voice over influence** — Build a distinctive point of view, not broad appeal

## What Defines Success

The editorial board succeeds when:
- Each issue reads as a coherent conversation, not a collection
- Readers notice patterns we're building across pieces
- We attract the best thinking on topics we care about
- We say things no one else is saying
- A year from now, readers remember us for our voice and vision

We're not running a content machine. We're building a magazine. A voice. A perspective on how design, AI, culture, and mystery intersect.

That's what the editorial board guards, develops, and publishes.
